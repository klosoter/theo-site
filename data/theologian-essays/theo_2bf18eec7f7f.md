### TITLE
A ressourcement Thomism of pure act: renewing classical metaphysics for Trinity, creation, and Christ in dialogue with modernity.

### PREVIEW NOTES
- **Context and setting**: American Dominican **Thomist** in the early twenty-first century, shaped by **Aquinas**, post-Vatican II **ressourcement**, and debates with **Barthian** and **analytic** theologies.
- **Method**: **sacra doctrina** grounded in **Scripture and Tradition**, aided by **Aristotelian-Thomist metaphysics** and the **analogia entis**, with careful use of **patristic ressourcement** and engagement with **contemporary philosophy of religion**.
- **Key works**: **The Analogy of Being** 2011; **The Incarnate Lord** 2015; **The Light of Christ** 2017; **The Trinity: On the Nature and Mystery of the One God** 2022.
- **Central categories**: **divine simplicity**, **pure act** and **participation**, **analogia entis**, **processions and relations of origin**, **inseparable operations and missions**, **creatio ex nihilo**, **hylomorphism**, **beatific vision**.
- **Interlocutors and influences**: **Thomas Aquinas**, **Garrigou-Lagrange**, **Cajetan**, **Balthasar** and **de Lubac**, **Barth** and the critique of the **analogia entis**, **analytic theists** like **Davies** and **Stump**, dialogue with **Palamas** on **deification**.
- **Reformed reception cue**: Strong convergence on **classical theism** and **Trinitarian orthodoxy**, tension over **analogia entis**, **nature and grace**, **created grace**, and **sacramental realism** vis-a-vis **Scripture’s principium** in **Reformed** and **WTS** traditions.

### ESSAY
Thomas Joseph White, O.P., is a leading contemporary **Thomist** whose work offers a comprehensive ressourcement of **Aquinas** for the Church amid modern intellectual turbulence. Formed within the **Order of Preachers** and committed to the unity of **sacra doctrina**, he articulates a renewed **classical theism** in conversation with **patristic** sources, the **Council of Nicaea** and its Trinitarian grammar, and the **Second Vatican Council’s** call for aggiornamento. His ministry has unfolded through academic leadership in the Dominican House of Studies in Washington, D.C., the international **Thomistic Institute**, and since 2021 as **Rector Magnificus** of the **Angelicum** in Rome. These institutional roles are integral to his theological program, since White sees doctrine, worship, and mission as inseparable, a conviction that structures his accounts of **divine simplicity**, **Trinitarian processions**, **Christology**, and **sacramental life**.

White’s method is unambiguously **Aquinas-centered**. He retrieves **ST I q.1** on **sacra doctrina** as a science ordered by **revelation** and nourished by **Scripture** and **Tradition**, while employing the **philosophia ancilla** as a servant. Against modern suspicion of metaphysics, he defends an **Aristotelian-Thomist** account of being and causality marked by **actus purus** and **participation**, integrated with the **analogia entis**. White’s hermeneutic is explicitly *ressourcement*: he reads **Athanasius**, **Augustine**, **Maximus the Confessor**, and **Thomas Aquinas** as a common doctrinal tradition that orients later debates with **Barth**, **Rahner**, **Balthasar**, and **analytic theists**. Theologically, he adopts a scholastic clarity that avoids dualisms between **nature and grace** by locating **creatio ex nihilo** and the **order of charity** in the one wisdom of God, while also honoring the gratuity of **grace** and the priority of **Christ**.

The hinge of White’s project is stated programmatically in **The Analogy of Being: Invention of the Antichrist or the Wisdom of God?** published in 2011. There he defends the **analogia entis** as the metaphysical grammar that protects **divine transcendence** while enabling true predication about God. Engaging **Karl Barth’s** 1930s rejection of the analogia entis, White argues that **analogy** arises from the metaphysical structure of **participation** whereby creatures have **esse** as a received act. This metaphysical reception grounds a moderate realism about **God-talk**, where names are said of God neither univocally nor equivocally but analogically. White’s interlocutors include **Erich Przywara** as a Catholic proponent, **Reginald Garrigou-Lagrange** as a Thomist guide, and modern critics who fear the analogia entis undercuts the primacy of revelation. White’s thesis is that **analogy** secures revelation’s intelligibility rather than usurping it, since the revealed names of God presuppose, purify, and elevate our natural knowledge of being. In this book he consolidates a suite of commitments that recur throughout his later works: **divine simplicity**, **aseity**, **immutability**, **impassibility**, and the patristic rule of **inseparable operations**.

White’s Christological center is developed in **The Incarnate Lord: A Thomistic Study in Christology** published in 2015. He retrieves **Chalcedon’s** definition of the **hypostatic union** and the patristic formula **anhypostasia-enhypostasia**, presenting **Christ** as one divine person with two complete natures. He defends the **communication of idioms**, the **uncreated personhood of the Word**, and the **created graces** proper to Christ’s humanity, including the **beatific vision** in the human intellect of Jesus according to **Benedict XII’s** dogmatic definition. Against modern **kenoticism**, **adoptionism**, and some strands of **Rahnerian** transcendental Christology, White argues that **divine immutability** is reconcilable with the real historical life of Jesus through **theandric operations**: the one person acting according to two natures. He integrates **soteriology** by retrieving **Anselm’s satisfaction** and **Aquinas’s** account of **sacrifice**, reading the Cross as an act of obedient charity that merits grace for the many. His biblical-theological method attends to **Hebrews**, **John**, and **Philippians 2**, interpreted via **Athanasius** and **Leo the Great**, and it is complemented by sacramental conclusions about the **priesthood** and **Eucharist**.

White’s synthetic catechetical vision appears in **The Light of Christ: An Introduction to Catholicism** from 2017. Here he unfolds **creation**, **covenant**, **Trinity**, **Christ**, **Church**, **sacraments**, and **last things** as a unified economy of salvation. Against post-Enlightenment fragmentation, he argues that **metaphysics** is necessary for doctrinal coherence. The chapters on **creation and science** are especially notable. White defends **creatio ex nihilo** while engaging the **Big Bang** and **evolution** as domains of **secondary causality** ordered under divine **primary causality**. He uses **hylomorphism** to argue for the **spiritual soul** as **subsistent form**, capable of immortality and beatitude. He critiques both crude **intelligent design** strategies that conflate efficient causes with final causality and reductive **scientism** that excludes formal and final causes. The book also articulates a robust **sacramental realism**, with the **Eucharist** as real presence grounded in **transubstantiation**, and a rich account of **deification** as created participation in the uncreated life of **Trinity** through **grace**, in conversation with **Gregory Palamas** and **Aquinas**.

White’s most comprehensive doctrinal monograph is **The Trinity: On the Nature and Mystery of the One God** published in 2022. He begins with **natural theology**, recovering **Aquinas’s Five Ways** in a contemporary register. The proofs from motion, causality, contingency, degrees of being, and teleology are read through the lens of **pure act**, **ipsum esse subsistens**, and **final causality**. White confronts **theistic personalism** and modal collapse worries, arguing for robust **divine simplicity** without sacrificing a real distinction of **relations of origin**. In the revealed doctrine proper, he retrieves **Augustine’s** and **Aquinas’s** accounts of **procession** by way of **intellect** and **will**, explicating **generation** of the **Word** and **spiration** of the **Spirit**. He expounds the **filioque** as a Western doctrinal development faithful to the patristic consensus. Central to his method are the rules of **inseparable operations** and **appropriation**, which enable reading Scripture’s economy without reducing the one God to three centers of consciousness. He correlates **missions** with **processions**, showing how the temporal sending of Son and Spirit manifests and communicates the eternal relations, while safeguarding divine freedom and the gratuity of **grace**.

Throughout these dogmatic treatments White engages **modern interlocutors**. To **Barth’s** critique of **analogy**, he replies that revealed names presuppose a created openness to being that does not diminish revelation’s sovereignty. To **Rahner’s rule** that economic Trinity is the immanent Trinity and vice versa, he offers a Thomist account that affirms the rule while insisting on metaphysical clarity concerning **processions** and **relations of origin**, lest the *ad intra* be collapsed into the *ad extra*. In conversation with **Balthasar**, he appreciates the dramatic portrayal of the **Paschal mystery** while warning against any language that suggests **immanent Trinitarian suffering** that would compromise **impassibility**. With **Palamas**, he argues for convergence on **deification** as participation in divine life through **created grace**, while respectfully delimiting the **essence-energies** idiom vis-a-vis **simplicity**. In dialogue with **analytic philosophy of religion**, he appreciates **Brian Davies** and **Eleonore Stump** for retrieving **classical theism**, while pressing critiques of **Plantinga** and **Swinburne** for latent univocity in models of divine attributes and providence.

The theological architecture of White’s work is metaphysical yet ecclesial. His Thomism is not a museum piece but a lived **sacra doctrina** enacted in **liturgy** and **mission**. Hence his attention to **sacramental metaphysics** and the **Church**. He defends **baptism** and **Eucharist** as efficacious causes of grace by divine institution, grounded finally in **Christ’s priesthood** and the **Trinitarian missions**. His ecclesiology is marked by communion with **Petrine** ministry, but it is equally patristic and evangelical in tone. His vision of Christian life culminates in the **beatific vision**, where the created intellect sees God in the light of **glory** with **charity** perfected. Importantly, the **beatific vision** in Christ’s human soul is not a rival to his historical growth but its formal principle, since the **grace of headship** overflows into the **members** by the Spirit. He converts speculative clarity into spiritual pedagogy, encouraging contemplative study that issues in preaching, a classic **Dominican** ambition.

White’s engagement with **science** and culture is marked by confidence in **secondary causality**. He argues that the natural order exhibits intelligibility as participation in **divine wisdom**, a conviction that licenses constructive dialogue with contemporary cosmology and biology. He defends **human uniqueness** against reductive physicalism by detailed accounts of **intellectual operations**, **intentionality**, and **free will**, drawing on **Aquinas’s De Veritate** and **ST I** to argue for the immateriality of the intellect. He offers a nuanced account of **providence** and **freedom**, rejecting both hard determinism and libertarianism that severs God’s causality from creaturely agency. His treatment of **evil** follows the Thomist privation doctrine, integrating pastoral sensitivity while preserving **divine goodness** and **simplicity**. This posture toward culture and reason exemplifies his larger strategy: retrieve **classical metaphysics** to heal modern ruptures in knowledge and life.

The best way to summarize White’s doctrinal development is to say that he is crafting a contemporary **Thomist synthesis** that binds together **natural theology**, **Trinity**, **Christology**, **creation**, and **sacrament**, with the **analogia entis** as the metaphysical hinge and **inseparable operations** as the hermeneutical rule. His distinctive contribution is a patient, ecumenically aware defense of **classical theism** that answers modern objections without surrendering the metaphysical hard edges of **simplicity** and **pure act**. He shows how **Aquinas’s** account of **esse** and **participation** illuminates exegetical questions about divine names, and how **Trinitarian relations** ground the missions that structure salvation history. He connects **Christ’s beatific knowledge** and **sacrifice** to the **sacraments**, and he demonstrates how **hylomorphism** secures a humane vision of persons against both materialism and disembodied spiritualism.

From a Reformed and Westminster Theological Seminary vantage, White’s project invites both admiration and principled critique. Reformed classical orthodoxy converges with his defense of **divine simplicity**, **aseity**, **immutability**, **inseparable operations**, and the pro-Nicene rule of **relations of origin**; it also welcomes his robust **Christology**, affirmation of the **beatific vision**, and careful articulation of **missions**. Yet the Reformed tradition raises cautions about the **analogia entis** as an epistemic preamble that can appear to grant metaphysics a principium independent of **Scripture**. The **nature and grace** architecture, especially the account of **created grace** as a habitus and **sacramental causality**, seems to threaten the primacy of the **covenant Word** and the forensic character of **justification** central to Reformed soteriology. Van Tilian concerns about autonomy in **natural theology** lead to a preference for a **covenantal analogy** ordered by **special revelation**, where all predication is ruled by **covenant lordship** rather than by an abstract **analogia entis**. Finally, while White’s retrieval of **deification** as participatory grace has points of contact with **union with Christ**, Reformed dogmatics would recast participation as federal and mystical union arising from the decree and mediated by **Word and Spirit**, not from an ontological habitus. A constructive Reformed alternative appropriates White’s **classical theism**, **Trinitarian grammar**, and **inseparable operations**, while subordinating philosophical claims more strenuously to the principium of **Scripture**, recalibrating **nature and grace** through **covenant**, and reframing participation through **union with Christ** within the economy of redemption.

### RECAP NOTES
- **Setting**: American **Dominican Thomist** active in the early twenty-first century, leading at the **Angelicum** and in the **Thomistic Institute**, retrieving **Aquinas** for post-Vatican II theology.
- **Core focus**: Renewing **classical theism** through **analogia entis**, **pure act**, and **participation**, integrated with **Trinity**, **Christology**, and **sacramental** life.
- **Major works**: **The Analogy of Being** 2011 defending **analogia entis**; **The Incarnate Lord** 2015 on **hypostatic union** and **beatific vision**; **The Light of Christ** 2017 catechetical synthesis; **The Trinity** 2022 on **processions**, **missions**, and **simplicity**.
- **Doctrinal tools**: **divine simplicity**, **aseity**, **impassibility**, **inseparable operations**, **relations of origin**, **hylomorphism**, **creatio ex nihilo**, **transubstantiation**, **deification**.
- **Interlocutors**: Dialogue with **Barth** on **analogy**, **Rahner** and **Balthasar** on **Trinity** and **Christology**, resonance with **Davies** and **Stump**, conversation with **Palamas** on **deification**.
- **Reformed assessment**: Convergence on **classical theism** and **Trinitarian orthodoxy**, tension over **analogia entis**, **nature and grace**, **created grace**, and priority of **Scripture** within **covenant** for **WTS**-style Reformed theology.
- **Legacy**: A leading voice in ressourcement **Thomism**, shaping academic and ecclesial discourse on **God**, **creation**, and **Christ**, and a durable reference point for ecumenical engagement with **classical theism**.
