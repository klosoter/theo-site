### TITLE
Naturalizing mind and meaning: from the intentional stance to Darwin’s universal acid

### PREVIEW NOTES
- **Harvard and Oxford formation** under **W. V. Quine** and **Gilbert Ryle** in the rise of **cognitive science** and late **analytic philosophy**, set against the post-1960s debates on **behaviorism**, **functionalism**, and **evolutionary theory**.
- **Methodological naturalism** with a **third-person functionalism**, using **heterophenomenology**, the **intentional stance**, and evolutionary **reverse engineering** to explain **mind**, **free will**, and **religion**.
- **Key works**: **Content and Consciousness** (1969), **Brainstorms** (1978), **Elbow Room** (1984), **The Intentional Stance** (1987), **Consciousness Explained** (1991), **Darwin’s Dangerous Idea** (1995), **Kinds of Minds** (1996), **Freedom Evolves** (2003), **Breaking the Spell** (2006), **Intuition Pumps** (2013), **From Bacteria to Bach and Back** (2017), **Just Deserts** (with **Gregg Caruso**, 2021).
- **Central terms**: **intentional stance**, **design stance**, **physical stance**, **multiple drafts model**, **heterophenomenology**, **real patterns**, **competence without comprehension**, **memes**, **cranes vs skyhooks**, **belief in belief**, **evitability** in **compatibilism**.
- **Interlocutors**: **Jerry Fodor**, **John Searle**, **Thomas Nagel**, **David Chalmers**, **Richard Dawkins**, **Stephen Jay Gould**, and religious critics like **Alvin Plantinga**; influences include **Ryle**, **Quine**, and **William James**.
- **Reformed reception cue**: challenge to **sensus divinitatis**, **imago Dei**, and **special revelation**; partial overlap with **Reformed compatibilism** but deep conflict over **teleology**, **normativity**, and the **noetic effects of sin**.

### ESSAY
Daniel C. Dennett, born in 1942 and deceased in 2024, pursued a single audacious program: to explain **mind**, **meaning**, **morality**, and **religion** as products of **natural selection** and **cultural evolution**, all without appeal to **skyhooks** or unearned explanatory lifts. He trained at **Harvard** with **W. V. Quine** and at **Oxford** under **Gilbert Ryle**, absorbing a late **analytic** sensibility wary of **Cartesian substance dualism** and dismissive of hidden essences. His career at **Tufts University** anchored a prolific engagement with **cognitive science**, **evolutionary biology**, and the philosophy of **mind**. The through-line from **Content and Consciousness** (1969) to **From Bacteria to Bach and Back** (2017) is the insistence that apparent **intelligence** can be produced as **competence without comprehension**, with **Darwin’s dangerous idea** acting as a universal acid to recalcitrant mysteries, including **religion**.

Formed by **Ryle’s** anti-Cartesianism and **Quine’s** naturalized epistemology, Dennett developed a method that is resolutely **methodologically naturalist** and **functional**. Early work in **Content and Consciousness** pressed the point that **mental content** is to be understood in terms of **functional roles** and patterns of **information processing**, not as occult **qualia**. The ambient climate after the eclipse of strict **behaviorism** and the rise of **computational theories of mind** gave Dennett both a caution against naive introspection and a framework in which to naturalize **intentionality**. For Dennett, **teleology** enters the story via **evolution**, not metaphysical design; and **epistemology** proceeds by **third-person** models and predictive success, not privileged **first-person authority**.

From that method flows his signature construct, the **intentional stance**, articulated programmatically in **The Intentional Stance** (1987). On this view one may adopt the **physical stance** to predict a system via physics, the **design stance** to predict it via its engineered function, or the **intentional stance** to predict it as if it had **beliefs** and **desires**. Dennett’s much-debated position is neither crude **instrumentalism** nor naive **realism**, but a pragmatist realism about **real patterns**. Patterns that yield strong compressions and predictive power, as in his 1991 paper **Real Patterns**, are real enough to underwrite talk of **beliefs**. Against **Jerry Fodor’s** robust representationalism, Dennett emphasizes that **content** is ascription-dependent and tied to **ecological rationality**, yet not arbitrary. The ontology of **intentional systems** is layered: the **intentional vocabulary** earns its place when it tracks system-level stabilities not evident from a mere **physical stance**.

Consciousness, the citadel of the **Cartesian theater**, receives his most famous deflation in **Consciousness Explained** (1991). Dennett proposes a **multiple drafts model** in which parallel, competitive processes in the brain yield a rolling **narrative center of gravity** rather than a metaphysical **self** watching inner screen-plays. The method is **heterophenomenology**, a stance that catalogs **first-person reports** without granting them incorrigible authority, integrating them with **neuroscience** and **cognitive psychology**. Interlocutors like **John Searle** and **Thomas Nagel** protest that something essential about **qualia** escapes such treatment, and **David Chalmers** frames the **hard problem** to insist that explaining functions leaves phenomenal feel untouched. Dennett responds that the very grammar of **qualia** is suspect and that once we model access, discrimination, and report, nothing explanatory remains to be done; the **Cartesian theater** is a user illusion, not an ontological datum.

Evolution provides Dennett his cosmological backdrop in **Darwin’s Dangerous Idea** (1995). He celebrates **natural selection** as a **universal acid** dissolving appeals to **skyhooks** and replacing them with bottom-up **cranes**, cumulative devices of construction that explain apparent **design**. He chastises **greedy reductionism** yet defends a responsible **unification** of explanations from **biology** to **culture**. Terms like **good tricks**, **arms races**, and **competence without comprehension** render the Darwinian grammar vivid. His defense of **memes** as cultural replicators aligns him with **Richard Dawkins** and against critics such as **Stephen Jay Gould**, who resist adaptationist stories in culture. By **Kinds of Minds** (1996) he refines an evolutionary graduation of **cognitive architectures**, ranging from basic **intentional systems** to language-using agents with **autocued rehearsal** and deep **counterfactual** capacities.

Dennett’s work on agency culminates in his compatibilist trilogy: **Elbow Room** (1984), **Freedom Evolves** (2003), and the dialogue **Just Deserts** with **Gregg Caruso** (2021). Against libertarians like **Robert Kane** and skeptics like **Galen Strawson** and **Derk Pereboom**, Dennett argues that **free will** worth wanting is a set of social and neurocognitive competences: foresight, **evitability**, sensitivity to **reasons**, and participation in **moral practices** that shape behavior. Evolutionary and cultural **scaffolding** build the agent-relative space of **responsibility**, which is justified consequentially by its **predictive** and **moral** payoffs. Though sometimes criticized for redefining **freedom**, Dennett insists that once we stop hankering after **metaphysical indeterminism**, the compatibilist account secures what matters: **accountability**, responsiveness to **norms**, and the capacity to learn to do otherwise in the sense of different outcomes under different **counterfactual** conditions.

Religion enters the frame explicitly with **Breaking the Spell** (2006), subtitled **Religion as a Natural Phenomenon**. Dennett proposes to naturalize **faith** by studying it with the full toolkit of **evolutionary psychology**, **anthropology**, and **cognitive science of religion**. Concepts like **belief in belief** capture the phenomenon of agents who value the social effects of being believers even when proximate **doctrinal content** is thin or unsettled. **Ritual**, **prayer**, and **taboos** are treated as **cultural technologies** selected for group coordination, **credibility enhancing displays**, and transmission efficiency through **memes**. Dennett’s plea is procedural: stop shielding **religion** from empirical inquiry and subject it to the same **heterophenomenology** that debunks the **Cartesian theater**. Critics from **Alvin Plantinga** to theologians of **religious experience** counter that this program begs the question against **special revelation** and fails to account for the irreducible **normativity** of worship, truth, and divine command.

The later synthesis, **From Bacteria to Bach and Back** (2017), weaves together **evolution**, **culture**, and **mind** via the rubric of **competence without comprehension**. Language, music, and mathematics are framed as **cultural ecosystems** whose **design** emerges from countless **cranes** rather than a **skyhook**. The idea of **intelligent design without a designer** is deployed to demystify **intentionality** and the **self** as a **center of narrative gravity**, an organizing fiction with enough **real pattern** to enter causal explanations. His procedural wisdom is distilled in **Intuition Pumps and Other Tools for Thinking** (2013), where he reheats and refines his **philosophical method**: thought experiments, **evolutionary heuristics**, and stance-taking across levels. The net effect is a unified **naturalistic teleology**, where purposes are genuine but historically scaffolded, and where **meaning** and **morality** ride on the back of **selection** and **cultural drift**.

Dennett’s polemical posture as one of the **New Atheists**, alongside **Dawkins**, **Hitchens**, and **Harris**, often overshadowed the subtlety of his **stance theory** and **real patterns** ontology. Yet even in polemic he sought to distinguish **greedy reductionism** from legitimate unification, to preserve the legitimacy of the **intentional stance** as explanatorily robust. His sustained debates with **Searle** over the **Chinese Room**, with **Chalmers** over **qualia**, and with **Gould** over **evolutionary explanations** of culture map a career-long defense of **cranes** against **skyhooks**. His method of **heterophenomenology** has influenced empirical programs in **neuroscience** and **cognitive psychology**, while his **compatibilism** shapes contemporary disputes about **moral responsibility**, legal policy, and the appropriateness of **retributivism** in a scientific age.

For theologians, Dennett’s work is a standing invitation to clarify what is meant by **soul**, **freedom**, **revelation**, and **teleology**. If **intentionality** is a **real pattern** sustained by **cultural practices**, the doctrine of **imago Dei** must either resist or appropriate that claim. If **religion** is a cluster of **memes** and **cultural technologies**, churches must ask what in their practice is mere **scaffolding** and what is **sacramental reality**. Even granting much to Dennett’s **reverse engineering** of creatures and cultures, any theological anthropology must face his charge that appeals to **mystery** are too often disguised **skyhooks**. The burden shifts to theology to show how **teleology** can be more than projection while remaining more than **Darwinian bricolage**, and how **normativity** can be more than an adaptive equilibrium of **coordination games**.

Reformed and Westminster evaluations can be succinct. First, Dennett’s **methodological naturalism** rules out from the outset **special revelation** and the **self-attesting authority** of Scripture, which a **Van Tilian** approach counts fundamental; his **heterophenomenology** cannot register the **sensus divinitatis** except as a datum to be explained away. Second, while his **compatibilism** overlaps structurally with **Reformed compatibilist freedom under providence**, it lacks the metaphysical depth of **divine concurrence** and the ethical normativity of **law** and **covenant**, reducing **responsibility** to consequentialist utility rather than to conformity to **God’s will**. Third, his **real patterns** account of **intentionality** and **norms** does not secure the objectivity of **truth** and **ought**, which in **classical Reformed** thought are grounded in the **triune Logos**, **imago Dei**, and a created order marked by **teleologia ad Deum**. A Reformed alternative would affirm much of Dennett’s **cranes** as **common grace** insights into creaturely mediation and **second causes**, while rejecting his ban on **skyhooks** by insisting that **providence** and **revelation** are not ad hoc gaps but the ontological and epistemic frame within which **nature** is intelligible. On this view, **evolutionary teleology** is subordinate to **covenantal teleology**; **belief in belief** is corrected by **faith in Christ**, and **competence without comprehension** is finally ordered by **wisdom** given *ad extra* by the **Spirit** through **Word** and **sacrament**.

### RECAP NOTES
- **Setting**: Late **analytic philosophy** and emergent **cognitive science** under **Quine** and **Ryle**; lifelong career at **Tufts** engaging **evolutionary theory** and the **mind-body problem**.
- **Core problem**: Naturalize **mind**, **meaning**, **morality**, and **religion** without **skyhooks**, using **Darwinian cranes** to replace dualist and theological explanations.
- **Major works**: **Content and Consciousness** (1969), **The Intentional Stance** (1987), **Consciousness Explained** (1991), **Darwin’s Dangerous Idea** (1995), **Freedom Evolves** (2003), **Breaking the Spell** (2006), **From Bacteria to Bach and Back** (2017).
- **Distinctive positions**: **intentional stance** and **real patterns** realism; **multiple drafts** model with **heterophenomenology**; **compatibilist free will** via **evitability**; **memetics** and **cultural evolution**; **competence without comprehension**.
- **Interlocutors**: Debates with **Fodor**, **Searle**, **Nagel**, **Chalmers**, **Dawkins**, **Gould**; exchanges with religious philosophers like **Plantinga** over **religion as natural phenomenon**.
- **Reformed critique**: Rejects **special revelation** and **sensus divinitatis**; thin grounding for **normativity** and **teleology**; partial formal overlap with **Reformed compatibilism** but lacking **providence** and **covenant**; invite a **common grace** appropriation of **cranes** under the **triune Logos**.
- **Legacy**: Canonical defender of **naturalism** in philosophy of **mind** and **religion**; enduring tools like the **intentional stance** and **intuition pumps**; a persistent foil for any theology of **imago Dei**, **freedom**, and **teleology** in an evolutionary age.
