### TITLE
Evangelical dogmatics as ressourcement: Word-and-Spirit theology for the church after Webster

### PREVIEW NOTES
- **Contemporary evangelical retrieval** within the post-Barth, postliberal moment; shaped by the **Websterian renaissance** in Anglophone dogmatics.
- **Ecclesially located dogmatics**, a **Word and Spirit epistemic stance**, and **theological interpretation of Scripture** as a disciplined, canonical science.
- **Theological Theology: Essays in Honor of John Webster** (2015, ed. with Darren Sarisky and Justin Stratis); **A Companion to the Theology of John Webster** (2021, ed. with Michael Allen); selected essays in **systematic theology** and **theological interpretation** across the 2010s–2020s.
- Central terms: **evangelical dogmatics**, **sanctified reason**, **creator-creature distinction**, **rule of faith**, **theological ontology**, **evangelical catholicity**.
- Interlocutors and influences: **John Webster**, **Karl Barth**, **Thomas Aquinas**, **Augustine**, **Kevin J. Vanhoozer**, **Michael Allen**, **Scott R. Swain**, **T. F. Torrance**.
- Reformed cue: appreciable alignment with **classical theism** and **Scripture as principium**; questions around **Barthian patterns** and **nature-grace** in a **Reformed scholastic** frame.

### ESSAY
#### 1. Formation, context, and method
R. David Nelson emerges as a theologian of the contemporary evangelical retrieval, a movement situated amid the long wake of **Karl Barth**, the revisionary legacy of **postliberalism**, and the constructive reappropriation of **classical theism** among younger evangelicals. The intellectual atmosphere that formed Nelson is marked by the re-centering of dogmatics around **John Webster**’s programmatic claims: theology as the **scientia Dei** undertaken coram Deo, the primacy of **revelation** and the **sanctification of reason**, and the renewal of **theological interpretation of Scripture**. Nelson’s work inhabits this Websterian renaissance, taking as methodological givens the **creator-creature distinction**, **the Word and Spirit** economy, and the churchly location of theology as a calling rather than a purely academic craft. The wager is that evangelical dogmatics can be both **catholic** and **confessional**, drawing on **Augustine** and **Aquinas** while attending to the evangelical emphasis on **Scripture** and **conversion**.

Nelson’s method is best described as **ecclesially located dogmatics** ordered by the **rule of faith** and executed as a mode of **sapiential judgment**. He treats theology as *theologia* in the classical sense, a habitus of the mind reordered by grace, rather than as merely a history of doctrines or religious studies. Epistemically, this involves confidence in **Scripture as principium cognoscendi** and the Spirit’s illumination, a chastened openness to metaphysical grammar under the pressure of **revelation**, and a refusal to sever **exegesis** from **dogma**. In Nelson’s hands, **theological interpretation of Scripture** is not a free-form homiletics but a disciplined engagement with the canon under the church’s **regula fidei**, animated by *analogia fidei* and a robust **canonical ontology**. This situates his thought within the broader evangelical strategy of ressourcement: a return to sources oriented to contemporary coherence rather than antiquarian nostalgia.

#### 2. Editorial leadership and intellectual positioning
Nelson’s distinctive contribution includes a strategic editorial vocation that has shaped recent Anglophone dogmatics. His co-editing of **Theological Theology: Essays in Honor of John Webster** in 2015 and the later **A Companion to the Theology of John Webster** in 2021 helped to codify the contours of Webster’s influence on **doctrine of God**, **Scripture**, and **holiness**. These volumes gathered voices across evangelical and catholic lines, thereby enacting the **evangelical catholicity** Nelson commends. They also advanced a programmatic agenda: dogmatics must be governed by **divine self-communication** and ordered to **God’s glory**, neither collapsing into historicism nor escaping into speculative rationalism.

This editorial posture clarifies Nelson’s own theological stance. He resists a disjunctive choice between **Barthian** and **Thomist** sensibilities, preferring a chastened metaphysics disciplined by **revelation** and a chastened christocentrism that honors **divine aseity**. His affinities with **Michael Allen** and **Scott Swain** are evident in the retrieval of **divine simplicity**, **impassibility**, and **immutability**, while he shares with **Kevin J. Vanhoozer** a hermeneutical attentiveness to **canonical form** and **theodramatic** participation. The net effect is an integrative posture: **classical attributes** are rearticulated with an evangelical accent, and **Scripture** is received not merely as text but as **instrumental cause** of knowledge of God in the Spirit’s economy.

#### 3. Scripture, exegesis, and dogmatics
At the heart of Nelson’s constructive reflection lies a coherent account of **Scripture and dogmatics**. Echoing **John Webster**, he operates with a duplex principium: the **principium essendi** of theology is the triune God in self-communicative grace, while the **principium cognoscendi** is **Holy Scripture** as the Spirit’s sanctified medium. Against the fragmentation of biblical studies from systematic reflection, Nelson advocates **theological interpretation** as the mode by which the church reads canonically and reasons *sub ratione Dei*. This is not a sacralization of method but a confession that the **canon** exists within God’s economy of **revelation** and that theological judgment is a matter of **spiritual discernment** within the **communio sanctorum**.

In practice, this yields a certain grammar for doctrinal construction. First, **Christology** is materially generative, yet never allowed to eclipse **theologia**: the **homoousios** confesses the Son’s full deity within the **simplicity** of God, thereby safeguarding robust **monotheism** while grounding the **economy**. Second, **creation** and **providence** are treated as good gifts, consistently ordered by the **creator-creature distinction** and illuminated by the canon’s doxological teleology. Third, exegesis is pursued under the **rule of faith**, attentive to **canonical sequencing** and to the economy’s internal order, so that doctrinal loci are reciprocally clarifying rather than hermetically sealed. Nelson thus embodies what he elsewhere promotes: **dogmatics as scripturally normed wisdom**, oriented to the church’s praise and catechesis.

#### 4. Doctrine of God and metaphysical retrieval
Nelson participates in the evangelical recovery of **classical theism**, a retrieval marked by recommitments to **divine simplicity**, **aseity**, and **immutability** alongside a renewed appreciation for **processions and missions**. The frame is consciously **Augustinian-Thomist**, though always set within the primacy of **revelation** and calibrated by **Barth’s** salutary cautions regarding speculative projection. For Nelson, classical attributes are not philosophical add-ons but the grammar that protects the **gospel’s ontology**: only the God who is **actus purus** can freely and graciously give himself without loss; only the simple God can be wholly present in each of the divine **opera ad extra** without partition.

Correspondingly, Nelson is attentive to the **Trinity’s taxis** and the distinction between *ad intra* processions and *ad extra* missions. He reads the **economy** as revelatory and fitting but not exhaustive of the **immanent life**. Such commitments stabilize **Christology** and **pneumatology**, preserving the **homoousios** and the full **Spirit’s deity** while resisting any drift toward **subordinationism** or **modalism**. The metaphysical retrieval therefore serves catechetical ends: to secure worship, to clarify the grammar of **salvation**, and to interpret the canon within the **divine simplicity** that anchors the unity of God’s acts.

#### 5. Creation, providence, and nature-grace
On **creation and providence**, Nelson prefers a doxological, teleological rendering. The **creator-creature distinction** is not a metaphysical gulf but the generative space of gift, hospitality, and participation by grace. Providence is described as the wise governance of the **triune God**, whose **impassibility** secures the reliability of divine love across history’s contingencies. Nelson resists both deistic caricatures and historicist collapses, commending instead a thick account of **providence** that embraces secondary causes and the moral drama of **sanctification**.

This yields a constructive posture on **nature and grace**. Nelson’s Websterian instincts elevate the reordering of reason by **sanctification**, yet he does not reject the **integrity of nature** nor the possibility of a modest **natural theology**. Rather than adjudicating the **analogia entis** as a single theory, he asks how evangelical confession can affirm **creaturely participation** in the divine gifts without compromising **divine freedom**. His constructive instinct is to specify the scope of **general revelation** christologically and pneumatologically, while safeguarding **grace’s gratuity**. The result is a chastened affirmation: nature is real and teleologically ordered, yet saving knowledge is irreducibly **revelatory** and **ecclesial**.

#### 6. Church, sacraments, and evangelical catholicity
Nelson’s ecclesial instincts are manifest in his advocacy of **evangelical catholicity**, a call for evangelical theology to inhabit the one church’s doctrinal legacy with gratitude and discernment. The **marks of the church** are treated with Reformation clarity, yet broadened by an appreciation for **patristic** and **medieval** wisdom. On **Word and sacrament**, Nelson emphasizes the priority of **preaching** as the instrument of **faith** and the sanctified reading of **Scripture**, while encouraging evangelical attentiveness to **Baptism** and **Eucharist** as instituted means of grace. The accent is pastoral and doxological: sacraments as sites where the **triune God** communicates Christ’s benefits in the Spirit’s power.

This ecumenical wideness is not thin. Nelson urges evangelicals to retrieve **creedal** and **conciliar** grammar, to read **Athanasius** and **Augustine** for **Trinity** and **Christology**, and to receive **Aquinas** for metaphysical clarity. Yet he resists flattening distinctives into lowest-common-denominator theology. Evangelicalism’s commitment to **conversion**, **mission**, and **Scripture’s authority** must not be traded for ecumenical ease. The constructive aim is a churchly dogmatics that is both rousingly **Reformed** in its biblicity and warmly **catholic** in its doctrinal habits.

#### 7. Dogmatics as witness: sanctified reason and theological judgment
Across his essays and editorial interventions, Nelson frames **dogmatics** as an act of **witness**. The theologian’s intellect is not neutral but ordered through **sanctification**, which yields the virtues of **patience**, **clarity**, and **courage** in doctrinal reasoning. This moral-epistemic orientation resists the domestication of theology into either **academic historicism** or **ideological apologetics**. It also specifies the task of **theological judgment**: gathering the canon’s many voices under **Scripture’s unity**, articulating doctrines with **conceptual modesty** and **metaphysical tact**, and ordering the whole toward **doxology**.

Nelson therefore views **theological interpretation of Scripture** not as a repudiation of historical exegesis but as its fulfillment under **revelatory** constraints. Historical tools are welcomed as ministerial aids, yet the canon’s primacy and the **Spirit’s agency** set the horizon. Dogmatic loci are then coordinated, not siloed. **Soteriology** presupposes **Christology** and **Trinity**; **ecclesiology** presupposes **revelation** and **sanctification**; **ethics** presupposes **creation** and **providence**. The result is a coherent, catechetically serviceable map of Christian teaching.

#### 8. Interlocutors: Barth, Webster, and the catholic tradition
Nelson’s dialogue partners chart the coordinates of his work. From **Karl Barth** he receives a chastened confidence in the primacy of **revelation**, the freight of **Christology**, and the warning against projectionist **natural theology**. From **John Webster** he gains the methodological heart of his program: **sanctified intellect**, **Scripture as canon**, **dogmatics as holy reason**, and a persistent call to theological chastity. From **Augustine** and **Aquinas** he learns the metaphysical grammar of **divine simplicity**, **aseity**, and **creation’s participation**. From **Vanhoozer**, **Allen**, **Swain**, and **T. F. Torrance** he absorbs hermeneutical and dogmatic resources for rendering evangelical theology both canonical and metaphysically articulate.

Yet these interlocutions are not simply additive. Nelson calibrates **Barth** with **Aquinas**, seeking a third thing: a **Reformed** and **evangelical** dogmatics in which the **rule of faith** structures exegesis, **classical theism** structures theology proper, and **mission** structures pastoral practice. The result is a measured constructive voice, marked by grammatical rigor, canonical attunement, and an avowed intention to serve the **church’s confession** in the present.

#### 9. Works, influence, and institutional mediation
Nelson’s co-editing of **Theological Theology: Essays in Honor of John Webster** in 2015 and the subsequent **A Companion to the Theology of John Webster** in 2021 are more than bibliographic entries. They signify a strategy: stabilize the gains of the **Websterian renaissance**, gather allies around **classical theism** and **Scripture’s primacy**, and resource a generation of pastors and scholars with constructive tools. His additional essays in **systematic theology** and **theological interpretation** across the 2010s–2020s further articulate the contours of an **evangelical dogmatics** that refuses the divorce of **church** and **academy**.

Equally significant is Nelson’s editorial leadership in evangelical publishing, where acquisitions and curation have advanced a coherent program: renewing **doctrine of God**, recovering **Trinitarian theology**, encouraging **biblical reasoning**, and nurturing **evangelical catholicity**. The practical fruit appears in the shape of curricula, pastoral catechesis, and academic discourse that increasingly assume **divine simplicity**, **Word and sacrament**, and **canonical exegesis** as normal theological furniture. Through these mediations, Nelson has helped to normalize a more **Reformed catholic** grammar amid broader evangelicalism.

#### 10. Integrated Reformed/WTS evaluation
A **Reformed** and **Westminster Theological Seminary** evaluation finds much to commend: Nelson’s alignment with **Scripture as principium**, his retrieval of **classical theism** including **divine simplicity** and **impassibility**, and his ecclesial commitment to **Word and sacrament** cohere with **confessional Reformed orthodoxy**. His Websterian stress on **sanctified reason** strengthens the Reformed emphasis on **illumination** and **the self-authenticating Word**, and his advocacy of **canonical exegesis** resonates with a covenantal reading of **redemptive history**. Tensions arise where **Barthian** pressures may attenuate **natural theology** beyond what classic **Reformed scholasticism** allowed, narrowing the **covenant of works** or the creational capacity for receiving general revelation. Similarly, a strong Christocentricity, if unguarded, can risk functionalizing **theologia** by **oikonomia**, placing strain on **autotheos** and the principled independence of theology proper. A Reformed alternative would press more explicitly the **covenantal ontology** that orders nature and grace, affirm a modest **theologia naturalis** within the creator-creature distinction, and clarify the asymmetry between *ad intra* and *ad extra* to buttress **immutability**. Even so, Nelson’s synthesis remains a salutary model of evangelical dogmatics under the **Word and Spirit**, offering an irenic yet robust path for confessional theology to inhabit the church’s present with classical depth and biblical clarity.
