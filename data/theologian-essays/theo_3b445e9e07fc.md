### TITLE
Retrieving classical theism for evangelical orthodoxy through Nicene and Reformed ressourcement in a Baptist key.

### PREVIEW NOTES
- **Twenty-first-century evangelicalism**, postconservative turbulence, and the **EFS controversy** prompt Barrett’s retrieval of **pro-Nicene** and **Reformed scholastic** doctrine of God.
- Method: **ressourcement** with **scholastic method**, **biblical reasoning** under the **rule of faith**, and qualified **Thomistic metaphysics** ordered to Scripture.
- Key works: **Salvation by Grace** 2013, **God’s Word Alone** 2016, **Reformation Theology** 2017 editor, **40 Questions About Salvation** 2018, **None Greater** 2019, **Simply Trinity** 2021, **The Reformation as Renewal** 2023.
- Central terms: **divine simplicity**, **aseity and actus purus**, **immutability and impassibility**, **eternal generation**, **inseparable operations**, **autotheos**, **sola Scriptura vs solo Scriptura**, **catholic Reformation**, **analogical predication**.
- Interlocutors and influences: **Augustine**, **Athanasius**, **Gregory of Nazianzus**, **Thomas Aquinas**, **Calvin**, **Turretin**, **van Mastricht**, **Bavinck**; allies **James Dolezal**, **Scott Swain**; critics and foils **Wayne Grudem**, **Bruce Ware**, **Jürgen Moltmann**.
- Reformed cue: alignment with **Westminster Confession 2** and **Reformed orthodoxy** on the doctrine of God, with debates over **natural theology** and **Van Til’s** covenantal epistemology.

### ESSAY
#### 1. Formation, context, and method
Matthew Barrett emerges within the ferment of late modern evangelicalism, where the combined pressures of **open theism**, **process theism**, and **social trinitarianism** unsettled catechesis on the **doctrine of God**. Trained in Baptist and evangelical institutions and now professor at **Midwestern Baptist Theological Seminary**, Barrett situates himself within the **Reformed** stream of evangelical theology while addressing a broader audience through **Credo Magazine** and the **Credo Podcast**. The immediate historical catalyst was the mid 2010s **Eternal Functional Subordination** debate, in which a functionalist account of the **Trinity** was normalized in popular systematics. Against this background, Barrett proposes a return to **pro-Nicene** grammar and **Reformed scholastic** dogmatics, making the case that classical Christian theism is neither an arid metaphysics nor a Catholic inheritance alien to Protestants, but the deep grammar of biblical confession that sustained **Athanasius**, **Augustine**, **Aquinas**, **Calvin**, and **Turretin**.

Barrett’s method is explicitly **ressourcement**, a retrieval of patristic and scholastic sources inside a canonical and confessional envelope, what he calls **biblical reasoning** under the **rule of faith**. He argues that **sola Scriptura** never meant **solo Scriptura**, that the Church’s creeds and catechisms serve a ministerial, not magisterial, role, and that well-ordered **metaphysics** clarifies rather than corrupts **exegesis**. His is a scholastic mode of inquiry, favoring careful distinctions, **analogical predication**, and a hierarchy of causes, all disciplined by the text of Scripture. He commends **Thomistic** categories such as **actus purus**, **simplicitas Dei**, and **analogia**, yet insists that such grammar be ruled by **canon** and **creed**, not by autonomous reason.

#### 2. Early works on Scripture and salvation
The early works exhibit a Reformed soteriological and bibliological center of gravity. In **Salvation by Grace** 2013 Barrett advances an Augustinian and **Reformed** account of **effectual calling** and **regeneration**, arguing that salvation is monergistic and that the **ordo salutis** hinges upon the priority of **grace** over human freedom. He contends, with **Calvin** and **Bavinck**, that the **Spirit’s** internal call creates what it commands, positioning **Arminian** synergism as a lapse into Pelagian tendencies. The argumentative posture is scholastic in form, marshaling Scripture, tradition, and reason in coordinated fashion to defend **unconditional election** and **irresistible grace**.

In **God’s Word Alone** 2016 Barrett reframes **sola Scriptura** against both Roman Catholic claims of magisterial parity and evangelical **biblicism**. The doctrine is rendered as a material-formal principle integrating the **rule of faith**, the norming role of **Scripture**, and the ministerial role of **tradition**. Barrett attends to the **Reformation** sources, showing how **Luther**, **Calvin**, and the **Westminster divines** combined exegesis, **creedal** consensus, and **scholastic method**. Here he introduces a leitmotif that will govern later dogmatics: the Reformers were not iconoclasts of the catholic tradition but its renovators, deploying classical tools within a new covenantal economy of authority.

#### 3. Reformation catholicity and editorial synthesis
As editor of **Reformation Theology** 2017 Barrett curated a systematic survey that signaled his thesis of Reformation catholicity. The volume positions **Reformed orthodoxy** not as a break from patristic and medieval theology but as its evangelical renewal, where doctrines like **divine simplicity** and **impassibility** are assumed as givens rather than innovations. The following popular-level **40 Questions About Salvation** 2018 consolidates these convictions at a catechetical register, rehearsing **union with Christ**, **justification by faith alone**, and **perseverance** within a robustly Augustinian schema. The editorial work displays Barrett’s commitment to churchly pedagogy, where recovering **confessional** norms guides evangelical identity.

This retrievalist thesis receives its programmatic historical defense in **The Reformation as Renewal** 2023. Barrett contends that the Reformation was a **renewal** of the one, holy, catholic, and apostolic church, not a repudiation of **Aquinas** or the **scholastic** tradition tout court. He argues that **Protestant scholasticism** is a disciplined extension of the **Reformation**, that **Calvin’s autotheos** does not sever the Son from **eternal generation**, and that **nature and grace** were ordered by the Reformers without capitulating to a two-tier ontology. This broad historiographical claim underwrites Barrett’s dogmatic retrieval: classical Christian metaphysics is not an alien imposition but Scripture’s rational service.

#### 4. The undomesticated God: attributes and simplicity
In **None Greater** 2019 Barrett prosecutes a full-scale return to **classical theism**, defending **aseity**, **immutability**, **impassibility**, **eternity**, **omniscience**, **omnipotence**, and above all **divine simplicity**. His polemical foil is what he calls the domestication of God, a modern tendency to imagine a God who changes, suffers, or reasons discursively with the world, characteristic of **theistic mutualism** and certain strands of analytic and open theism. With **Aquinas** and **Turretin**, Barrett affirms that God is **actus purus**, without potency, and that the divine attributes are identical with the divine essence, eschewing composition in God. **Impassibility** is not apathy but plenitude of life, such that God’s love is uncreated and immutable, not a reactive pathos. The result is a coherent account in which divine perfections mutually entail one another, not a list of properties accumulated.

The book is didactic and polemical, clarifying **analogical predication** and ruling out univocal attributions to God. Barrett insists that biblical anthropomorphisms are accommodated idiom, grounding this claim in **Augustine’s** and **Athanasius’s** rules of reading. He reframes problematic proof texts by coordinating **Scripture with Scripture** and by reading **ad intra** and **ad extra** distinctions into exegesis. Thus **immutability** does not preclude God’s ad extra relations to creatures, nor does **impassibility** deny the suffering of the incarnate Son **secundum humanitatem**. The Creator-creature distinction is the controlling axiom, generating a Reformed identitarian account of divine names.

#### 5. Simply Trinity: processions, missions, and anti-subordinationism
The centerpiece of Barrett’s retrieval is **Simply Trinity** 2021, a popular and yet carefully sourced defense of **Nicene** and **pro-Nicene** trinitarianism. He argues that the doctrine must be received through the classical sequence **processions**, **relations of origin**, **simplicity**, and **inseparable operations**, then read back into the **economy of salvation** via the **missions** of the Son and Spirit. Against **EFS**, Barrett contends that any eternal role subordination collapses into ontological subordination or implies multiple wills in God, violating **simplicity** and **consubstantiality**. He retrieves **Gregory of Nazianzus’s** taxis and **Augustine’s** psychological analogies only insofar as they serve the core Nicene insight that personal distinctions are by relations of origin, not by degrees of authority.

Barrett recovers **eternal generation** as the hinge between **ad intra** and **ad extra**. The Son is from the Father according to His personal mode, not as a creature but as **homoousios**, and the Spirit proceeds from the Father and the Son, **filioque**, in Western dogmatics. The **inseparable operations** principle guards the unity of the divine will in all ad extra works, while **appropriations** explain why some works are fittingly ascribed to one person. On this basis, the **missions** reveal but do not constitute the persons. Barrett’s reading draws from **Lewis Ayres** and **Gilles Emery**, while remaining tethered to **Calvin**, **Turretin**, and **Bavinck**, stitching together patristic grammar and Reformed confessionalism.

#### 6. Autotheos and the Son’s aseity
A recurring test case is **Calvin’s autotheos**, the affirmation that the Son is God from Himself with respect to essence, while eternally begotten with respect to person. Barrett argues that this is not a contradiction but a layered claim shaped by **simplicity** and **relations of origin**. Essence is not communicated as a part but is identical to Godhead, thus the Son’s deity is not derivative in a compositional sense. The Son’s personal origin, **generatio**, is from the Father, which does not introduce ontological subordination or dependence in the composite sense. Barrett enlists **Turretin** and **van Mastricht** to show that **autotheos** safeguards **aseity** without undermining **eternal generation**, integrating standard **Reformed scholastic** distinctions into contemporary evangelical discourse.

This approach is deployed to counter the evangelical trope that authority and submission are primordial features of Trinitarian life. Barrett insists that **hierarchicalism** within the immanent Trinity is incompatible with **simplicity** and **immutability**. Authority and submission are features of the incarnate **Son’s** mediatorial office, **status exinanitionis**, not eternal properties of divine life. The result is a retrieval of the **Athanasius** to **Aquinas** to **Turretin** line that reads the **gospels** and **Pauline** Christology through the lens of patristic metaphysics and Reformed confessions.

#### 7. Scripture, metaphysics, and the rule of faith
One of Barrett’s distinctive contributions is the rescripting of **sola Scriptura** as an engine for **creedal** and **scholastic** retrieval. He claims that the Bible teaches realities that require metaphysical grammar, such as **simplicity** and **impassibility**, and that abandoning such grammar regresses to unruled proof-texting. **Sola Scriptura** therefore yields **ressourcement**, not bare **biblicism**. Barrett leverages **Bavinck’s** organic motif, where Scripture and dogma coinhere, and he reads **Westminster Confession 1** as sanctioning subordinate standards that guide exegesis. The ancient **rule of faith** sets the horizon of biblical reading, making **Nicene** dogma the hermeneutical baseline for Trinitarian passages rather than a post hoc overlay.

Barrett is not shy about constructive **metaphysics**. He contends that evangelical suspicion of **Aquinas** is misplaced, and that the **Reformation** refined rather than rejected medieval ontology. The program in **The Reformation as Renewal** is to render the Protestant retrieval of **Aquinas**, **Bonaventure**, and the **Cappadocians** plausible, so that evangelical theology can speak credibly about **God**, **creation**, and **providence** without capitulating to either analytic univocity or postliberal indeterminacy. The accents are methodological as much as doctrinal, inculcating a habitus of slow reading under **creed** and **canon**.

#### 8. Polemics and partners in retrieval
Barrett’s polemical engagements are sharp but ecumenical in posture. He resists **Moltmann’s** social trinitarianism and correlational method where divine suffering becomes a metaphysical necessity, marking a departure from **impassibility** and **simplicity**. He critiques popular evangelical systematics represented by **Wayne Grudem** and **Bruce Ware** where eternal roles of authority and submission are posited in the Godhead. He also resists aspects of analytic theism where **divine simplicity** is reduced or reconstructed to accommodate possible worlds logic. Alongside **James Dolezal** and **Scott Swain**, he assembles a coalition of retrieval theologians who argue that **classical theism** is the catholic heritage of Protestants, not a foreign body to be rejected.

Central to this coalition is the pro-Nicene principle of **inseparable operations**. Barrett shows how this principle is confessed across **Augustine**, **Athanasius**, **Aquinas**, **Calvin**, and **Bavinck**, and how it undercuts both EFS and social trinitarian models that envision distributive agency. He presses that the **missions** reveal the **processions**, so the sending of the Son and Spirit ad extra do not instantiate eternal taxis of authority. The result is a disciplined trinitarianism that is immune to contemporary projections and returns evangelical theology to the grammar of **creed**, **catechism**, and **confession**.

#### 9. Doctrinal synthesis and pedagogical strategy
Barrett’s corpus displays a coherent doctrinal synthesis. **None Greater** secures **classical attributes**, **Simply Trinity** clarifies **processions** and **missions**, **God’s Word Alone** defines the **epistemic posture**, and **The Reformation as Renewal** legitimates **ressourcement** historically. The doctrinal center is **divine simplicity** as the architectonic principle, cascading into **immutability**, **impassibility**, **aseity**, **eternal generation**, and **inseparable operations**. The soteriological stack in **Salvation by Grace** and **40 Questions About Salvation** presupposes this God and orders **effectual calling**, **union with Christ**, and **perseverance** accordingly. The pedagogy is repetitive by design, a catechetical retrieval that normalizes difficult terms for lay and clergy alike.

His strategy includes rehabilitating suspect terms within evangelicalism. **Simplicity** is recovered as a safeguard of divine unity, **impassibility** as the ground of uncreated love, **eternal generation** as the grammar of the Son’s relation, and **autotheos** as a Reformed hedge against subordinationism. Barrett repeatedly appeals to **Turretin** and **van Mastricht** to demonstrate a continuous Protestant line, and to **Aquinas** and **Gregory** for catholic reach. His teaching project institutionalizes retrieval through **Credo**, sponsoring essays and interviews that model disciplined **exegesis** under classic dogma.

#### 10. Reformed and WTS evaluation
From a **Reformed** and **Westminster Theological Seminary** vantage, Barrett represents a salutary retrieval of **Westminster Confession 2** on **simplicity**, **immutability**, and **Trinity**, and a timely corrective to **EFS** and evangelical **biblicism**. His insistence on **analogical predication**, **inseparable operations**, and the **processions missions** pattern accords with **Turretin** and **Bavinck** and coheres with a covenantal reading of Scripture that centers **Christ’s mediatorial office** rather than projecting it **ad intra**. Two tensions warrant note. First, while Barrett’s qualified embrace of **Thomistic metaphysics** is exemplary in subordinating reason to **revelation**, a WTS-inflected **Van Tilian** critique will caution that any robust appeal to **natural theology** and the **actus purus** schema must be relentlessly normed by the **Creator creature distinction** as a covenantal, not neutral, epistemic axiom. The fear is not **Aquinas** per se, but an abstract nature grace schema that could threaten the principial necessity of **special revelation** for true theological knowledge. Second, exegesis that foregrounds **simplicity** and **impassibility** must continue to show, as Barrett aims to do, how these doctrines elevate rather than mute the **covenantal reciprocity** of the living God who truly relates to His people **ad extra** without change **ad intra**. Within classical Reformed orthodoxy these concerns can be resolved by sharpening the ministerial use of **scholastic distinctions**, articulating a more explicit **covenantal ontology**, and sustaining the **analogia fidei** as regulative of metaphysical speech about God. On balance Barrett’s project strengthens evangelical and Reformed catechesis, restores theological confidence in **Nicene** and **Reformed scholastic** grammar, and invites a fruitful conversation between **Thomistic ressourcement** and **Van Tilian** covenantal theism.
