### TITLE
God without measure: retrieving classical theism for the sanctification of theology, Scripture, and reason.

### PREVIEW NOTES
- **Historical context**: late twentieth- and early twenty-first-century British academy, Anglican priest-scholar amid **postcritical hermeneutics** and **Barthian resurgence**, 1955–2016.
- **Method**: **theological theology** grounded in **revelation**, the **sanctification of reason**, and a dogmatics ordered from **theologia** to **oikonomia**.
- **Key works**: **Eberhard Jüngel: An Introduction** 1986; **Barth’s Moral Theology** 1998; **Karl Barth** 2000; **Word and Church** 2001; **Holiness** 2003; **Holy Scripture: A Dogmatic Sketch** 2003; **Confessing God** 2005; **The Domain of the Word** 2012; **God Without Measure** 2015.
- **Central terms**: **divine simplicity**, **aseity and immutability**, **non-competitive agency**, **principium theologiae**, **creaturely participation**, **theological ontology**.
- **Interlocutors**: **Karl Barth**, **Eberhard Jüngel**, **Thomas Aquinas**, **Augustine**, **Herman Bavinck**, **Oliver O’Donovan**; critics in **modern historicism** and **secular reason**.
- **Reformed cue**: strong alignment with **Reformed scholastic retrieval** and **Calvin’s autotheos**, qualified by Anglican **ecclesiology** and a reticent account of **inerrancy**.

### ESSAY
#### 1. Formation, context, and the proposal of theological theology
John Webster emerged as a leading contemporary Anglican dogmatician who insisted that theology be generated from God’s self-communication rather than from religious experience or cultural theory. In an academy shaped by **postcritical hermeneutics**, **liberal historicism**, and renewed **Barthian** interest, he proposed **theological theology**: a science formed by **revelation**, proceeding under **sanctifying grace**, and ordered to **doxology**. Educated at Cambridge and ordained in the Church of England, he taught at Durham, Wycliffe College Toronto, Oxford as **Lady Margaret Professor of Divinity** from 1995 to 2003, Aberdeen from 2003 to 2013, and St Andrews from 2013 until his death in 2016. As founder of the **International Journal of Systematic Theology** in 1999 he helped consolidate an international conversation about **dogmatics** as a coherent discipline. From early engagements with **Eberhard Jüngel** and **Karl Barth** to later retrievals of **Thomas Aquinas** and **Augustine**, Webster navigated the passage from a modern Protestant critique of metaphysics to a chastened, **classical theism** that he called theology’s natural grammar.

Webster’s opening wager was that **method** is not neutral. The very possibility of knowledge of God depends on the **principium theologiae** grounded externally in **Holy Scripture** and internally by the **Spirit’s illumination**. The intellect is not autonomous. It must be regenerated, its operations healed and elevated through **sanctification** so that it can confess the **perfections of God**. Therefore dogmatics is a work of **holy reason**, a practice of the church that is truthful because it participates in the **divine economy of grace**. Against the anxious instrumentalization of theology for culture or politics, he insisted on the priority of **theologia**: the contemplation and description of God **in se** before and as the ground of his works **ad extra**.

#### 2. Early Barthian phase and the turn to ecclesial dogmatics
Webster’s early scholarship on **Jüngel** and **Barth** established his instinct to think from the **Word of God** rather than from religious consciousness. The monograph **Eberhard Jüngel: An Introduction** in 1986 introduced anglophone readers to the ontological force of **God’s being as event**, while **Barth’s Moral Theology** in 1998 argued that Christian ethics flows from reconciliation, not natural law or social contract. In **Karl Barth** 2000, a lucid exposition, Webster accented Barth’s anti-speculative polemic and the centrality of **divine self-positing in Jesus Christ**. Yet even here he began to relocate Barth’s impulses within a broader metaphysical horizon, preparing the retrieval of **divine simplicity** and **non-competitive agency** that would mark his mature work.

The collections **Word and Church** 2001 and **Confessing God** 2005 crystallize an ecclesial dogmatics in which the church’s public witness arises from the **mission of the Word**. For Webster, the church is not a sociological datum but a **creature of the Word**, sanctified for **praise**, **hearing**, and **confession**. The pathos of these volumes is liturgical. Doctrine is ordered to **doxology**, and exegesis is a spiritual discipline of **hearing Scripture** as the **address of the triune God**. The bishop’s crozier and the professor’s pen both serve the same **ministerial authority** of the Word, a basic Anglican sensibility that nonetheless pressed toward catholic substance rather than via media compromise.

#### 3. Holy Scripture: sanctified creature and principium cognoscendi
With **Holy Scripture: A Dogmatic Sketch** 2003 Webster provided a programmatic bibliology that refuses reduction to either modern historicism or fundamentalist defense. Scripture is a **sanctified creature**, pressed into service by the **Holy Spirit** to administer the **saving presence** of the **Word**. The unity of **canon** is an effect of **divine providence** and **ecclesial reception**, not an arbitrary imposition. As the church reads, the **Spirit** effects a correspondence between text and reader that yields **knowledge of God**. The text is divinely commissioned yet creaturely, a fitting instrument of **revelation** that invites both **dogmatic location** and patient **grammatical-historical exegesis**.

Webster’s signal move is to restore **dogmatics** as the field in which Scripture’s status is explicated. He argues through the classic distinction of **principium cognoscendi externum** and **internum**, locating **Scripture** and **Spirit** as the external and internal principles that render theology possible. Errant construals of **inspiration** as a pre-dogmatic property of texts are displaced by a richer ontology of **divine use** and **ecclesial reading**. While he affirmed the **truthfulness** and **veracity** of Scripture, he transposed debates about **inerrancy** into the wider sphere of Scripture’s sanctified function in the **economy of salvation**, insisting that bibliology is a subset of **theology proper** and **economy**, not an apologetic firewall.

#### 4. Holiness and the perfections of God
In **Holiness** 2003 Webster re-orients Christian morality by beginning with the **divine perfection** of holiness as God’s radiant self-presence. Creaturely holiness is derivative participation in the **holy God**, not autonomous ethical achievement. The result is a tight pattern: **God’s holiness** grounds **sanctification**, which orders **vocation** and **obedience**. Central here is the axiom that God’s relation to creatures is **non-competitive**. Because the **triune God** is **ase**, simple, and immutable, God’s giving does not diminish creaturely integrity but constitutes it. Holiness is both separateness and abundant nearness, the consecrating claim by which God sets apart a people for **joyful praise**.

This attention to the **perfections of God** matured into Webster’s late retrieval, summed in **God Without Measure** 2015. He advances the thesis that **theology proper** must be restored as dogmatics’ architectonic core, since the **gospel** announces not primarily human well-being but the **plenitude of the triune life**. To confess **divine simplicity**, **immutability**, **impassibility**, and **aseity** is not to restrain biblical language but to clarify its ontological referent. The names of God are analogical but real, and the **doctrine of the Trinity** orders all divine works without collapse of **theologia** into **oikonomia**. This is a chastened metaphysics generated by revelation, not a pagan scaffold draped in Christian words.

#### 5. Creation, providence, and non-competitive agency
Webster developed a distinct **theological ontology** of the **God-world relation** in which **creatio ex nihilo** is a judgment of both gratuity and asymmetry. God gives being as an overflow of **triune goodness**; therefore creation is neither necessary emanation nor arbitrary fiat. The created order is teleologically directed to **beatitude**, and the **intellect** is ordered to truth by **grace elevating nature**. Providence is not a competing causal line but the transcendent cause that enables and perfects creaturely causes, a classic statement of **non-competitive agency**. In essays on **perfections** and **providence**, he redeploys scholastic distinctions such as **actus purus**, **communicable and incommunicable attributes**, and **fittingness** to describe the seamless glory of God’s action.

Anthropology and sanctification unfold within this ontology. Creatures participate in God’s goodness by grace, which heals the noetic and moral wounds of sin. The **sanctification of reason** is therefore integral to **soteriology**. The regenerate intellect learns habits of **theological judgment**, disciplining itself by Scripture and theology’s canon of **first principles**. Far from quietism, this yields a spacious account of creaturely flourishing and cultural labor, yet one policed by the priority of **divine instruction**. Webster’s **theological ethics** thus refuses secular moral theory’s autonomy while avoiding fideism through a thick doctrine of **creation**.

#### 6. Christology, reconciliation, and sanctification
Though he never wrote a full Christology, Webster’s essays articulate a sharply classical **two-natures** account with a strong **extra Calvinisticum**. The person of the **Son** assumes human nature without contraction of divine life. The **communication of idioms** is secured within the unity of the person, and the missions of the Son and **Spirit** enact the eternal **processions**. In the **work of Christ**, reconciliation is the theatre in which the **righteousness of God** becomes the life of sinners. Webster’s **soteriology** moves from the perfection of the **mediator** to the sanctified existence of the church, tying **atonement** and **sanctification** in a single movement of divine bestowal.

This Christological center grounds his emphasis on **holiness** as participation. The ethical shape of salvation is not add-on but the very form of communion with the **triune God**. In preaching and sacrament the **Word** re-enacts the reconciliatory address, rendering hearers obedient and joyful. The accent on **divine agency** ensures that grace is not cooperative synergy but unilateral gift that creates new correspondence. Here Webster stands close to **Reformed** accounts of monergistic grace while retaining his Anglican sense of ecclesial form and **sacramental** mediation.

#### 7. Church, Scripture, and the domain of the Word
The clusters **Word and Church** and **The Domain of the Word** 2012 sketch an ecclesiology of the **ministerial Word**, the church existing to hear, confess, and announce **divine instruction**. The **magisterial** is systematically subordinated to the **ministerial**, since authority resides in **revelation** administered by **Scripture** and the **Spirit**. Preaching is a human act annexed to the divine act, a particular instance of **non-competitive agency** in which God makes creaturely signs transparent to himself. The **sacraments** are part of this sanctified instrumentality, not rivals to the **Word** but distinct modes of **Christ’s self-gift**.

In Webster’s hands, ecclesial practices are not primarily sociological markers but exercises in **holy reason**. Catechesis trains the mind in **theological grammar**; liturgy orders desire to **divine perfections**; pastoral care is discernment of **sanctification**. The church is sent into the world not to translate itself into immanent rationalities but to offer witness from the **domain of the Word**. This does not reject cultural studies, but it relativizes them under the rule of **theologia**, a rule that guards the church from captivity to late-modern **secular reason**.

#### 8. The culture of theology and the sanctification of intellect
Webster’s lectures later published as **The Culture of Theology** distilled his convictions about the intellectual life. Theology requires a **culture**, a set of practices, institutions, and virtues animated by **Scripture** and **prayer**. The scholar is not a neutral analyst but a **hearer of the Word**, whose craft is a mode of **sanctification**. This theme returns across essays on **theological judgment**, the disciplining of **imagination**, and the ethics of **intellectual labor**. The result is a distinct pedagogy: theology is learned in a school of **praise**, sustained by habits of **repentance** and **joy**, and aimed at articulate **confession**.

Crucially, this view issues in a proposal for **theological metaphysics**. Rather than borrowing categories from alien systems, dogmatics, grounded in **revelation**, generates its own metaphysical idiom capable of stating **divine simplicity** and **aseity** without collapsing into univocity or equivocity. The **analogical** stretch of language is secured by participation: God makes himself known and, in making known, refashions language to bear him. Thus Webster vindicates **classical theism** not by cultural nostalgia but by evangelical necessity.

#### 9. Interlocutors, development, and legacy
Across his career Webster conversed with **Barth** and **Jüngel** while increasingly mining **Aquinas**, **Augustine**, and **Reformed orthodoxy** such as **Bavinck**. He did not become a Thomist of the schools. Rather, he appropriated **Thomas** as a grammar through which **revelation** could purify and perfect philosophical speech. He engaged **modern hermeneutics** without capitulation, arguing that the church’s reading of **Scripture** is properly spiritual and doctrinal. He differed from **Radical Orthodoxy** in refusing grand cultural programs, preferring a chastened ecclesial focus. As editor, teacher, and mentor he formed a generation of theologians committed to **theology proper**, **theological exegesis**, and **dogmatic rigor**.

His mature settlement, encapsulated in **God Without Measure**, influenced Anglican, Reformed, and Catholic readers who were searching for a post-critical, post-liberal way of saying **God** with metaphysical seriousness. By his death in 2016 he had sketched, though not completed, a full-scale **systematic theology**. The lines of influence are evident in current work on **divine perfections**, **Scripture’s ontology**, and **sanctified reason**, where Webster’s cadences about **non-competitive agency** and **theologia** now sound almost axiomatic.

#### 10. Reformed and WTS evaluation
From a classical **Reformed** and Westminster Theological Seminary vantage, Webster’s achievement is a monumental clarification of **theology proper** and **bibliology** as internally ordered to **revelation** and **sanctification**. His recovery of **divine simplicity**, **aseity**, and **immutability**, joined to a robust account of **Scripture** as **principium cognoscendi**, converges with **Calvin’s autotheos**, **Turretinian** principia, and **Bavinck’s** theocentric dogmatics. His insistence on **non-competitive agency** corrects voluntarist and personalist tendencies that can erode the Creator-creature distinction. Yet tensions remain. First, his reticence to use the grammar of **inerrancy**, while not denying **truthfulness**, can make his bibliology vulnerable where confessional Reformed theology seeks sharper claims about Scripture’s verbal perfection under **inspiration**. Second, his prioritization of **theologia** over **oikonomia**, though salutary, risks muting the covenantal form of revelation explicated by **federal theology**, especially the structural clarity of **covenant of works** and **covenant of grace** for reading **redemptive history**. Third, Anglican **ecclesiology** and sacramental accent sometimes leave underdeveloped the ministerial reciprocity of **Word and sacrament** framed by **covenant** as elaborated in Reformed standards. A Reformed alternative would receive Webster’s metaphysical retrieval, intensify his strong account of **Scripture** with explicit verbal **inerrancy**, and relocate his doxological dogmatics within the architecture of **covenant theology** so that **theologia** and **oikonomia** meet in the history of **God’s covenantal self-communication**. Even with these caveats, Webster’s corpus offers a profound ally for Reformed dogmatics seeking to confess the **triune God** with reverent boldness.
