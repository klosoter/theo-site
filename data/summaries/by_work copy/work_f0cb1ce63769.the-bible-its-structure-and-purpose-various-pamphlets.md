### TITLE
The Bible: Its Structure and Purpose (various pamphlets) - C.I. Scofield

### OUTLINE NOTES
- **Setting or publication context:** Early 20th century American evangelical/fundamentalist milieu; follows John Nelson Darby and Plymouth Brethren roots; coincides with rise of modern dispensationalist literature and the 1909/1917 popularity of the Scofield Reference Bible.
- **Central thesis or doctrinal problem:** The Scriptures are best read as a divinely ordered sequence of administrations (dispensations) that reveal God's unfolding purposes; correct interpretation requires a literal, historical-grammatical hermeneutic that maintains a clear distinction between Israel and the Church.
- **Core argument or structure:**
 - The Bible is organized into successive dispensations - distinct economies in which God tests human obedience and progressively reveals revelation.
 - Each dispensation has a specific purpose within God's overarching plan to glorify Christ and govern human history.
 - A literal interpretation of prophecy yields a futurist, premillennial eschatology and expects a distinction between prophetic promises to Israel and the present Church age.
 - Church practice, soteriology, and ethics are to be understood in light of the present dispensation rather than by collapsing all biblical commands into a single covenantal system.
- **Figures, sources, interlocutors engaged:** Builds on Darby and 19th-century dispensationalists; engages implicitly and polemically with covenant theologians (Reformed tradition, Westminster standards) and historic premillennial/preterist alternatives.
- **Doctrines treated:** Revelation and hermeneutics, covenant and dispensations, Christology (centralizing Christ's future kingdom), ecclesiology (Church as parenthetical), soteriology (faith and grace within dispensational context), eschatology (premillennialism, rapture concepts), typology versus allegory.
- **Closing line:** The pamphlets synthesize a popular dispensational hermeneutic: Scripture is a progressive, administratively ordered revelation culminating in Christ's literal kingdom and requiring a literal reading that preserves Israel-Church distinctions.

### DISTINCTIVES
- **Unique emphases or formulations:**
 - Systematic partitioning of redemptive history into discrete dispensations with practical rules for reading each.
 - Strong insistence on literal, historical-grammatical exegesis applied especially to prophecy.
 - The Church as a distinct, interim economy not to be conflated with Israel's prophetic promises.
- **Corrections or contrasts to predecessors or rivals:**
 - Opposes classical covenant theology which reads Scripture through a single covenant of grace and tends to spiritualize prophecy.
 - Moves beyond Darby by popularizing and institutionalizing dispensational categories in lay-accessible formats (pamphlets, study notes).
- **Conceptual or terminological innovations:**
 - Codification of "dispensation" as central hermeneutical category for organizing Scripture.
 - Clear phraseology distinguishing administrations, promises, and prophetic fulfillments.
- **Reception and influence in Reformed and wider theology:**
 - Massive influence among American fundamentalists and conservative evangelicals; shaped popular eschatology, missions, and Bible-study methods.
 - Criticized by Reformed and covenantal scholars for disjunctions in covenant, ecclesiology, and ecclesial continuity; sparked sustained polemics with progressive covenantalism and neoâ€‘Reformed critiques.
- **Enduring value for study and teaching:**
 - Provides a concise, teachable model of dispensational hermeneutics; indispensable primary source for understanding 20th-century evangelicalism and the rise of literalist prophecy reading.

### KEY TERMS & USES
- **Dispensation:** An administration or economy in which God governs humans by specific responsibilities and revelations.
- **Literal interpretation:** Priority of a plain historical-grammatical reading, especially for prophecy.
- **Progressive revelation:** The idea that God gradually disclosed truth through successive dispensations.
 - Contrast: In progressive covenantalism, progressive revelation emphasizes covenantal continuity and unfolding of one covenantal plan rather than discrete administrative breaks.
- **Israel vs. Church distinction:** Theological separation of national Israel's prophetic promises from the Church's present role.
- **Premillennialism:** Christ returns before a literal thousand-year reign; central to Scofield's eschatology.
- **Rapture (implied):** Expectation of a distinct removal of the Church prior to full eschatological judgment (popularized in dispensational circles).
- **Prophetic literalism:** Tendency to read Old Testament prophecy as predicting concrete, future events for Israel and nations.
- **Administration:** Synonym for dispensation; denotes rules, responsibilities, and tests in a given period.
- **Typology:** Use of types and figures, but subordinate to literal prophecy; types point forward to Christ within dispensational framework.
- **Covenant (administrative sense):** Treated as arrangements within dispensations rather than a single unifying covenant of grace.
- **Hermeneutic (historical-grammatical):** Methodology stressing context, grammar, and authorial intent.
- **Purpose of Scripture (Scofield's emphasis):** Revelation of God's plan for government, testing, and the establishment of Christ's kingdom.

- **How this work is used across loci:**
 - As a **definition source** for classical dispensational categories and terminology.
 - As a **polemic** and contrast point in debates with covenant theology and progressive covenantalism.
 - As an **exegetical model** for literalist prophecy reading and for constructing study-Bible notes that shaped popular evangelical Bible study.
