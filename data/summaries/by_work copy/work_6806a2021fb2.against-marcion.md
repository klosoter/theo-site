### TITLE
Against Marcion (Adversus Marcionem) - Tertullian (c. 208-212 CE)

### OUTLINE NOTES
- **Setting / publication context**
 - Early 3rd-century Roman Church; Marcionism a major heresy proposing a canonical and theological rupture between Old and New Testaments.
 - Part of wider formation of Christian canon, apologetic responses to Gnosticism and docetism, and debates on Scripture-authenticity.
- **Central thesis / doctrinal problem**
 - Marcion posits two gods (a just creator of the Law and a good Father revealed in Christ) and rejects the Old Testament and large portions of Luke/Paul. Tertullian argues this fractures revelation and contradicts apostolic witness: there is one sovereign God who progressively reveals himself in history and Scripture.
- **Core argument / structure**
 - Five-book polemic: detailed textual and theological refutation of Marcion's edited Luke and Pauline corpus; systematic dismantling of Marcionite dualism.
 - Textual/exegetical move: demonstrates that Marcion's Luke and Paul's letters are mutilated and inconsistent with the received tradition.
 - Historical/canonical move: appeals to ecclesial reception and apostolic usage as criteria for textual authenticity.
 - Theological move: defends unity of God, continuity between Law and Gospel, genuine incarnation and atonement, and providential authorship of creation.
 - Antidote to docetism: affirms Christ's real human descent and suffering against Marcion's denials.
- **Figures, sources, interlocutors**
 - Principal interlocutor: Marcion of Sinope. Implicitly interacts with other currents (Gnostics, proto-Valentinians) and uses Pauline corpus, Luke, Old Testament, and patristic exemplars (apostolic tradition).
- **Doctrines treated**
 - Revelation (unity/continuity), Canon formation, Christology (incarnation, historicity of flesh), God (monotheism vs dualism), Creation & providence, Law vs Grace, typology, and implicitly anthropology (origin and transmission of human nature).
- **Closing line**
 - A sustained defense of the unity of God and Scripture: true Christianity insists on one covenantal God who both creates and redeems, and on an apostolically received canon that interprets the Old in light of the incarnate Christ.

### DISTINCTIVES
- **Unique emphases**
 - Rigorous combination of textual criticism, canonical argument (ecclesial reception), and dogmatic contention - a forensic method rare in such intensity among early Latin writers.
 - Vivid polemical style: rhetorical invective paired with systematic argumentation.
- **Corrections / contrasts to predecessors or rivals**
 - Direct, sustained correction of Marcionite dualism and selective redactionism; rejects any theological justification for excising the Old Testament.
 - Moves beyond mere apologetic assertion to a proto-historical claim: documents are authenticated by apostolic and ecclesial usage.
- **Conceptual / terminological innovations**
 - Early deployment of a canonical-criterial argument (use = authenticity) that anticipates later patristic appeals to reception.
 - Clear linking of canon, Christology, and soteriology as mutually sustaining theological loci.
- **Reception and influence**
 - Instrumental in shaping orthodox anti-Marcionite tradition and in defending the legitimacy of the Old Testament as Christian Scripture.
 - Widely read in patristic and medieval traditions; influential for later debates on canon and continuity. Tertullian's later Montanism complicated his reception, but Adversus Marcionem remained a reference against dualistic heresies.
- **Enduring value**
 - Model for integrating textual-historical critique with doctrinal defense; valuable for teaching on canon formation, anti-heretical method, and the theological interdependence of Old and New Testaments.

### KEY TERMS & USES
- **Marcionism / Marcion**
 - Dualistic system: strict distinction between the OT demiurge (lawgiver) and the NT Father (gospel).
- **Demiurge**
 - Marcionite term for a creator god of justice; Tertullian rejects any separation between creator and Father.
- **Canon (ecclesial reception)**
 - Criterion: apostolic and church usage authenticate texts; Tertullian uses reception-history as proof against Marcion's edits.
- **Mutilated Luke / Edited Pauline Corpus**
 - Charge that Marcion deliberately excised passages that link Christ to the OT, betraying non-apostolic redaction.
- **Unity of God**
 - Central polemical point: one God who is creator, lawgiver, and redeemer.
- **Typology / Fulfillment**
 - OT events and institutions read as anticipations fulfilled in Christ - key hermeneutical tool against Marcion's dismissal of the OT.
- **Incarnation / Real Flesh**
 - Insistence on Christ's genuine human descent and suffering as historical and salvific (counter to docetic tendencies).
- **Providence & Creation**
 - Creator God is the author of history; continuity between creation and redemption rejects Marcionite disparagement of the world.
- **Law vs Gospel**
 - Reinterprets Law in light of Christ rather than discarding it; emphasizes continuity, not opposition.
- **Textual criticism**
 - Early instance of manuscript-comparison and authenticity-claim based on content and reception.
- **Polemic style**
 - Combines forensic argument with rhetorical invective; a hermeneutic and rhetorical model for later anti-heretical writing.
- **Anthropology / Traducianist inference**
 - While not the main focus, Tertullian's stress on Christ's real human origin supports his broader tendencies toward the transmission of human nature (traducianism), relevant to Traducianism vs Creationism debates.
