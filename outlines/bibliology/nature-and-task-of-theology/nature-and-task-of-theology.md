### TITLE
Theology as **doxological science** under **holy Scripture**

### PREVIEW NOTES
- Time and dogmatic location: **prolegomena within Bibliology**, ordered to **theology proper** and practiced for **ecclesial doxology**.
- Core question: What is theology’s **nature and task** as a churchly **science of God** that serves worship and mission.
- Principal figures and schools: **Augustine on doctrine and charity**, **Aquinas on sacra doctrina**, **Calvin on duplex principium**, **Schleiermacher on feeling and method**, **Barth on revelation and Word**, **Old Princeton on inerrancy**, **Kuyper and Bavinck on principia**, **Van Til on antithesis**, **Plantinga on Reformed epistemology**.
- Key concepts and loci connections: **revelation and Scripture**, **principium essendi and principium cognoscendi**, **fides quaerens intellectum**, **theologia and oikonomia**, **analogia fidei**, **law and gospel**.
- Major texts, events, or works with dates: **Augustine De doctrina christiana 397**, **Aquinas Summa 1265–1274**, **Calvin Institutes 1536–1559**, **Westminster Confession 1646–1648**, **Schleiermacher 1799**, **Barth Church Dogmatics 1932–1967**, **Vatican II Dei Verbum 1965**.
- Reformed and alternative trajectories: **Old Princeton confessionalism**, **Dutch Neo-Calvinist principia**, **Westminster federal theology**, alongside **Roman Catholic ressourcement**, **Lutheran theologia crucis**, **modern correlation**, **analytic theology**.
- Legacy cue or continuing debate: the status of theology as **public truth**, the **normativity of Scripture**, and the **theological interpretation of Scripture** amid plural modern methods.

### ESSAY
#### 1.**Definition & Dogmatic Location**
Christian theology is the **church’s sapiential science** that attends to God’s self-communication in **revelation and Scripture** for the praise of the Triune God and the nurture of the saints. In classic terms it is *scientia fidei*, **faith seeking understanding**, ordered to the **vision of God** and practiced within the body of Christ. Its principium essendi is the **triune Lord who reveals**, and its principium cognoscendi is **holy Scripture**, read in the Spirit and within the rule of faith. The nature of theology is thus intrinsically **ecclesial and doxological**, yet it remains rational, argumentative, and accountable to reality. The task of theology is to hear, confess, and teach the gospel with conceptual clarity and pastoral tact, that God may be glorified and the church edified.

The dogmatic location of this topic is **prolegomena**, seated within **Bibliology** and orienting every locus of the system. As prolegomena, theology sets forth its sources, norms, and ends, clarifying how **Scripture, tradition, reason, and experience** coinhere under the primacy of the canon. It explains the grammar of **revelation and faith**, the unity of *theologia* and *oikonomia*, and the ordering of doctrine to **catechesis and mission**. The task language is not neutral method talk but a participation in **the economy of grace**, since the Spirit unites the church to Christ and thus renders theology possible and fruitful.

#### 2.**Biblical Foundations**
Scripture grounds theology by presenting itself as **Spirit-breathed canon**, the norm that norms all teaching. Second Timothy 3.16 names the writings as *theopneustos*, which functions as a charter for **doctrine, reproof, correction**, and training in righteousness. First Peter 3.15 calls believers to give a reason for the hope within, an apologetic mandate that frames theology as **confessional reasoning**. Deuteronomy 6.4–9 charges Israel to confess and teach the Shema, a matrix for **catechesis and doxology**. Psalm 19 and Romans 1.19–23 affirm **general revelation**, yet Romans 10 ties saving knowledge to the **preached Word of Christ**, locating theology in the ministry of proclamation. John 1 and Hebrews 1 insist that the fullness of revelation is the **incarnate Son**, whose words and works are attested in the apostolic Scriptures for the church’s faith and understanding.

The Bible also instructs theology’s posture and method. Luke 24 shows the risen Christ opening the Scriptures to disclose the **Christological unity of the canon**, modeling a hermeneutic of **theological interpretation** governed by the gospel. Ephesians 4 presents ministry gifts for the building up of the body into the **measure of Christ**, so theological teaching must aim at unity in truth and love. The Pastoral Epistles call leaders to **guard the deposit**, implying a normative rule of faith that links **Scripture and tradition** under apostolic authority. Proverbs names wisdom as **fear of the Lord**, so theology is both intellectual and sapiential, receiving God’s address in humility and returning speech in **praise and obedience**.

#### 3.**Historical Development**
The patristic period forged a vision of theology as **biblical wisdom for charity**. **Irenaeus** articulated the **rule of faith** as the scriptural narrative summarized for baptismal confession. **Athanasius and the Cappadocians** fought for **homoousios** against Arianism in 325 and 381, demonstrating theology’s duty to clarify the **identity of God** for the sake of worship. **Augustine’s De doctrina christiana** near 397 defined theology as ordered reading that leads to **love of God and neighbor**, distinguishing the use of signs from the enjoyment of God. Early creeds and councils formed a habit of **dogmatic exegesis**, where interpretation is secured by the church’s confession of the triune name.

In the medieval period, **Aquinas** described sacra doctrina as **science from divine revelation**, a habitus that reasons from and about the articles of faith. Theology proceeds by **exegesis and argument**, drawing on *analogia fidei* and the service of **philosophy as handmaid**. **Bonaventure** stressed the ascent of the soul to God and the **unity of wisdom**, while the Sentences tradition schooled theologians in **systematic loci**. The university consolidated theology as a **public discipline**, while monastic schools held its **contemplative orientation**, keeping together speculation and devotion.

The Reformation repositioned theology under the material norm of **justification by faith** and the formal norm of **Scripture alone**. **Luther** advanced the **theologia crucis**, a grammar of the Word that finds God hidden in the cross and binds theology to the **law and gospel** distinction. **Calvin’s Institutes** 1536–1559 expounded the **duplex principium** of **God and Scripture**, tied to the work of the **Spirit’s internal testimony**, and located knowledge of God within **union with Christ**. The post-Reformation scholastics like **Turretin** 1679–1685 refined **principia, loci, and method**, while confessions such as the **Westminster Confession** 1646–1648 codified a canonical rule for **teaching and discipline**.

Modernity provoked crises and recalibrations. **Schleiermacher’s Speeches** 1799 and Christian Faith 1821 reconceived theology around the **feeling of absolute dependence**, organizing doctrine by **religious consciousness**. **Ritschl** reframed dogma as **value judgments** for the moral kingdom. **Barth’s Church Dogmatics** 1932–1967 restored emphasis on **revelation and the Word of God**, insisting that theology listens to the **self-speaking God**. **Vatican II’s Dei Verbum** 1965 articulated a **Scripture–tradition symphony** under the Word of God, renewing Catholic prolegomena. **Postliberal theology** re-centered the **ecclesial narrative** and **rule of faith** as the grammar of doctrine, while late twentieth century developments pressed **analytic clarity** and **theological interpretation of Scripture**.

#### 4.**Reformed Doctrine (Old Princeton / Dutch Neo-Calvinist / Westminster)**
Old Princeton construed theology as **science under inerrant Scripture** for the service of the church and the academy. **Charles Hodge** 1871–1873 and **B. B. Warfield** 1881–1921 defended **verbal plenary inspiration**, arguing that Scripture is the **normative cognitive principium**, and that theology gathers biblical facts by **inductive reasoning** into coherent system. While sometimes accused of **propositionalism**, Old Princeton joined **piety and scholarship**, producing a disciplined account of **revelation and doctrine** anchored in confessional orthodoxy.

Dutch Neo-Calvinism developed **principia theology** with emphasis on **worldview and antithesis**. **Abraham Kuyper’s Stone Lectures** 1898 and **Herman Bavinck’s Reformed Dogmatics** 1895–1901 affirmed **God as principium essendi** and **Scripture as principium cognoscendi**, yet stressed **common grace** and **sphere sovereignty** for cultural engagement. Their project held together **catholicity and confessionalism**, welcoming the service of **science and philosophy** while maintaining **Scripture’s primacy**. Westminster theology unified these streams under **confessional standards**, while **Geerhardus Vos** advanced **biblical theology** as a redemptive-historical discipline, and **Cornelius Van Til** insisted on **presuppositional apologetics** grounded in the **self-attesting Christ of Scripture**. The Reformed picture is thus a family resemblance of **canonical norm, covenantal frame, and ecclesial task**.

#### 5.**Alternative Accounts (Roman Catholic / Lutheran / Modern / Analytic)**
Roman Catholic theology construes its nature as **sacra doctrina within the church’s magisterium**, with **Scripture and tradition** as a single sacred deposit of the Word of God. **Dei Verbum** 1965 speaks of **God’s self-communication** in deeds and words intrinsically linked, read within the **living tradition**. Debates over **analogia entis** and **nature and grace** shape Catholic prolegomena, with **ressourcement and Thomist** streams recovering patristic and medieval wisdom for contemporary theology. **Joseph Ratzinger** and **Balthasar** stress **revelatory form and beauty**, while **Lonergan** proposes a **transcendental method** oriented to conversion of mind and heart.

Lutheran theology orients the task around **law and gospel** and the **theologia crucis**, understanding theology as **ministry of the Word** that delivers Christ’s benefits. **Melanchthon’s Loci** and the **Formula of Concord** 1577 frame doctrine as **evangelical consolation**, judging methods by their capacity to preach **justification by faith** and to honor the **real presence** of Christ in word and sacrament. The Lutheran profile thus prioritizes **kerygma and liturgy**, warning against speculative intrusions that eclipse the **cross and promise**.

Modern Protestant alternatives include **liberal correlation** and **neo-orthodox retrieval**. **Schleiermacher** makes theology a **practical science** of the church’s piety, while **Tillich** develops a **method of correlation** between existential questions and theological answers. **Bultmann** proposes **demythologization** to render kerygma intelligible to modern hearers, sparking disputes about **history and faith**. Against these, **Barth** relocates theology under the triune **self-revelation**, the **threefold form of the Word**, and the priority of **exegesis and confession**. Analytic theology advances **clarity and argumentative rigor** in service of the church, with **Plantinga and Wolterstorff** defending **Reformed epistemology** and warrant, while recent analytic dogmatics pursues **divine attributes, atonement models**, and **Trinitarian ontology** with renewed philosophical care.

#### 6.**Current Debates and Objections**
Debates persist over theology’s **scientific status** and its public intelligibility. Some challenge the **normativity of Scripture** amid historical criticism, proposing **plural canons** or a reconstructed rule of faith, while others retrieve **canonical and figural reading** that honors the literal sense and the **Christological unity** of Scripture. There are disputes over **inerrancy and infallibility**, with Old Princeton legacies contending for truthfulness of the canon in all it affirms, and others distinguishing **reliability for salvation** from broader historical claims. The rise of **theological interpretation** has renewed confidence that exegesis and dogmatics belong together in the **economy of the Word**.

Further questions concern **contextual theologies** and the catholicity of doctrine. **Liberation, womanist, feminist, and decolonial** voices press theology to hear the **cries of the oppressed**, reexamine **power and tradition**, and frame doctrine as **participation in God’s justice**. **Pentecostal and global South theologies** foreground **experience of the Spirit** and **narrative witness**, challenging Western rationalisms while affirming **biblical authority**. Digital media and the knowledge economy generate pressures toward **speed and fragmentation**, prompting retrieval of **monastic patience** and **ecclesial accountability**. Through all, the core objection remains whether theology can be both **confessional and public**, to which many reply by articulating **confident humility** that reasons from revelation toward the common good.

#### 7.**System Links (Trinity / Christology / Anthropology etc.)**
The nature and task of theology are indexed to **Trinity and revelation**. If God is triune communion, then theology participates in **hearing the Father in the Son by the Spirit**, and its methods must be **Christocentric and pneumatological**. Christology supplies the **mediator and content** of knowledge, since the **incarnate Word** is the hermeneutical key to Scripture and the economy. Anthropology frames the **noetic effects of sin** and the healing of intellect through **regeneration and sanctification**, while ecclesiology locates theology in the **communion of saints** under ordained ministries. The doctrine of Scripture names **canon, inspiration, and illumination**, while natural theology addresses **creational signs** in light of the gospel. Eschatology clarifies the **pilgrim status** of theology, a now and not yet between **faith and sight** that disciplines its claims and hopes.

#### 8.**Pastoral and Liturgical Payoff**
Rightly grasped, theology serves **preaching, catechesis, and sacrament**, equipping shepherds to teach the whole counsel of God with **clarity and consolation**. Confessional norms and doctrinal catechisms shape **Christian formation**, guarding the flock against **heresy and error** and nourishing **holiness and mission**. In worship, theology tunes the church’s voice to **lex orandi lex credendi**, where orthodox prayer trains **orthodox belief** and the people taste the **mystery of Christ**. In pastoral care, theology provides **wise judgment**, applying **law and gospel** to afflict the comfortable and comfort the afflicted, and offering **apologetic confidence** amid cultural bewilderment.

The task is also missional and public. Theology articulates **public truth** in humility, seeking the **common good** through truthful speech about God and neighbor. It resources discernment against **ideology and idolatry**, exposing rival liturgies and teaching the church to live as a **contrast society**. It emboldens intercession, protest, and peacemaking through the **Psalms and prophets**, and it funds resilient hope by fixing hearts on the **resurrection and the kingdom**. Such payoffs are not optional applications but the **very telos** of theology’s nature as **praise-ordered understanding**.

#### 9.**One concise closing sentence.**
Theology is the church’s **Spirit-led wisdom** that thinks God’s **revelatory Word** for the sake of **truthful worship and holy love**.
