# Kevin J. Vanhoozer (1957–)

Kevin J. Vanhoozer addresses the debate between **analogia entis** (analogy of being) and **analogia fidei** (analogy of faith) by rooting theological knowledge in **revelation** and **Scripture** rather than metaphysical speculation. He insists that knowledge of God is possible only through God's **self-communication** in the **Trinitarian economy**, emphasizing the primacy of **divine speech** and the centrality of Christ.

- "The analogia fidei is not a humanly constructed bridge to God but a divinely established correspondence between God’s communicative action and our receptive understanding." (Remythologizing Theology)

## Key Works

- Remythologizing Theology: Divine Action, Passion, and Authorship
- The Drama of Doctrine: A Canonical-Linguistic Approach to Christian Theology
- First Theology: God, Scripture & Hermeneutics
- Biblical Authority after Babel: Retrieving the Solas in the Spirit of Mere Protestant Christianity
- Is There a Meaning in This Text? The Bible, the Reader, and the Morality of Literary Knowledge

## Key Theological Commitments

1. **Primacy of Divine Speech**
    - God reveals himself through **speech acts** in Scripture, not through metaphysical participation.
    - The analogy between God and humanity is grounded in communicative action, not shared being.
    - Revelation is fundamentally **personal** and **dialogical**.
    - The **Trinitarian God** speaks, acts, and relates, making himself known in history.
    "God’s being is communicative: God is who he is in the act of speaking and making himself known." (Remythologizing Theology)

2. **Critique of Analogia Entis**
    - The analogia entis risks grounding theology in **natural theology** and philosophical abstraction.
    - It can obscure the **uniqueness of revelation** in Christ and Scripture.
    - Vanhoozer warns against seeking God through analogies of being apart from God's self-disclosure.
    - He stresses the **epistemic distance** between Creator and creature apart from revelation.
    "The analogia entis, if left unchecked, can become a ladder of metaphysics that bypasses the cross." (Remythologizing Theology)

3. **Affirmation of Analogia Fidei**
    - True knowledge of God comes by faith, in response to God’s initiative.
    - The analogy is established by God’s **gracious self-revelation** in Christ and Scripture.
    - Faith is the proper mode of knowing, not speculative reason.
    - The analogia fidei safeguards the **sovereignty of revelation** and the **centrality of Christ**.
    "The analogia fidei is the correspondence between God’s Word and the faith that hears and obeys." (First Theology)

4. **Canonical-Linguistic Approach**
    - Meaning and knowledge of God are mediated through the **canonical Scriptures**.
    - The church’s doctrine arises from attentive listening to the biblical drama.
    - The analogy between God and humanity is shaped by the **grammar of Scripture**.
    - Theological method must be rooted in the **textual and narrative** nature of revelation.
    "Doctrine is the church’s way of directing its performance of the scriptural drama." (The Drama of Doctrine)

5. **Trinitarian Hermeneutics**
    - Understanding God analogically requires attention to the **Trinitarian structure** of revelation.
    - The Spirit enables believers to participate rightly in the communicative action of God.
    - The analogia fidei is not static but dynamic, shaped by the ongoing work of the Triune God.
    - Hermeneutics is a spiritual discipline, not merely a technical exercise.
    "The Spirit’s role is to bring about the correspondence between the divine Word and human understanding." (Remythologizing Theology)

## Legacy and Impact

Vanhoozer’s work has significantly shaped contemporary **Reformed theology** by re-centering the analogia fidei as the proper framework for knowing God. His emphasis on **divine speech** and the **canonical-linguistic approach** has influenced theological method, hermeneutics, and doctrine. By critiquing the analogia entis and advocating for a Trinitarian, revelational epistemology, Vanhoozer has contributed to ongoing debates about the nature of theological knowledge and the authority of Scripture. His thought continues to inform discussions on the relationship between philosophy, theology, and biblical interpretation.
