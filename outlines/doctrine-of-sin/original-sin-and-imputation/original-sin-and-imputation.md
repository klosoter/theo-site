### TITLE
In Adam and in Christ: the justice of inherited guilt and the promise of imputed righteousness

### PREVIEW NOTES
- Time and location: **Augustinian hamartiology** from 412–418 through **Reformation federalism** in 1646–1648 to **modern analytic revisions** after 1950.  
- Core question: Can **original sin and imputation** be rendered coherent, just, and biblically faithful, and how do **federal headship and union with Christ** secure both?  
- Principal figures: **Augustine against Pelagius** at Carthage 418; **Aquinas on reatus and concupiscence**; **Trent Session V** 1546; **Luther and Calvin** on total depravity; **Turretin and Westminster** on imputed guilt; **Old Princeton immediate imputation** via Hodge and Warfield; **Bavinck and Dutch Neo-Calvinism** on organic solidarity; **Barth and Eastern Orthodoxy** resisting inherited guilt; **Oliver Crisp and analytic proposals** on models of participation.  
- Key concepts: **original guilt and corruption**, **federal headship and covenant of works**, **mediate versus immediate imputation**, **union with Christ and the second Adam**, **concupiscence and baptismal regeneration**.  
- Major texts and events: **Romans 5:12–21 and 1 Corinthians 15:22**; **Psalm 51:5 and Ephesians 2:3**; **Council of Carthage** 418 and **Orange** 529; **Trent** 1546 and **Westminster Confession** 1646–1648; **Edwards on Original Sin** 1758; **Bavinck Reformed Dogmatics** 1895–1901.  
- Reformed and alternatives: **Westminster federalism** and **Old Princeton immediate imputation** versus **Roman Catholic concupiscence without intrinsic guilt**, **Lutheran corruption with condemnation**, **Eastern ancestral sin** without imputed guilt, and **Barthian rejection of imputational schemas**.  
- Legacy and debate: **historical Adam and evolution**, **fairness of corporate culpability**, and the **Christological parallel of imputed righteousness** remain live dogmatic and pastoral issues.

### ESSAY
#### 1.**Definition & Dogmatic Location**
Original sin names the state of humanity after Adam, involving both **original guilt and original corruption**, with guilt reckoned to all in Adam and corruption inhering in our nature inclined to sin. **Imputation** is the forensic act by which God accounts Adam's first sin to his posterity and, by parallel, accounts **Christ's righteousness** to believers. The classic Reformed account locates this in **federal headship and covenant of works**, by which Adam stood as a public person who, upon transgressing, brought condemnation and death upon all whom he represented.

Dogmatically, the locus stands at the junction of **anthropology and soteriology**, while relying on **Christology** for the Adam–Christ parallel and on **covenant theology** for its legal-ontological structure. To deny imputation undermines the grammar of **justification by imputed righteousness**, while to deny corruption severs the need for **regeneration by the Spirit**. The doctrine therefore functions as a hinge holding together **sin's universality** and **grace's sufficiency**.

#### 2.**Biblical Foundations**
Genesis 2–3 narrates a historical probation in which Adam, fashioned *imago Dei*, breaks divine command and incurs the threatened death. The genealogical note that Adam begets a son in his own image in Genesis 5:3 suggests a postlapsarian **image now marred**, aligning with **Ephesians 2:3**, which speaks of humans as by nature children of wrath. **Psalm 51:5** confesses sin from conception, read by the Augustinian tradition as pointing to a **sinful nature and original culpability** rather than mere maternal sin.

The heart of the biblical case is **Romans 5:12–21**, where Paul frames a dual solidarity. The debated phrase *eph ho pantes hemarton* in verse 12 is naturally translated "because all sinned," yet the argument unfolds with the stronger thesis that through the **one man's trespass the many died**, and that through the **one man's disobedience the many were constituted sinners**. The forensic term in verse 19, *katestathesan*, resonates with **constituted status** rather than mere imitation, while the symmetry with Christ's obedience points to **imputation by federal representation**. **1 Corinthians 15:22** adds the blunt maxim, in Adam all die, in Christ all will be made alive, integrating **death's universality** with **resurrection's gift**.

Paul's logizomai language in Romans 4 secures the category of **reckoning or imputation** in justification, which provides the proximate analogue for reading Romans 5 as a **forensic-ontological solidarity**. The end of the passage, Romans 5:18, speaks of condemnation to all in Adam and justification leading to life to all in Christ, thus enshrining the double headship motif in **covenant representation**. The biblical storyline therefore maximizes both **corporate identity** and **Christological remedy**.

#### 3.**Historical Development**
Early fathers like **Irenaeus** emphasized **recapitulation**, reading Adam and Christ as two heads with the second undoing the first. **Tertullian's traducianism** sought an ontological account for the transmission of corruption, indicating an instinct toward **real solidarity** beyond mere imitation. The decisive turn came with **Augustine against Pelagius** between 412 and 418, arguing from Romans 5 that infants require baptism because they bear **original guilt**, not simply corrupted habits. **Council of Carthage 418** condemned Pelagian denial of inherited sin; **Orange 529** anathematized semi-Pelagian synergism, while not canonically fixing every detail of **imputation**.

Medieval reflection, especially **Aquinas on reatus and concupiscence**, stabilized the distinction: the guilt of original sin is removed in baptism, yet **concupiscence remains** as fomes peccati and inclines toward sin. Scholastic debates prosecuted the mode of transmission by **propagation not imitation**, in **Trent Session V** 1546, while denying that **concupiscence is itself sin** in the baptized. The **Eastern Orthodox** trajectory emphasized **ancestral sin**, receiving mortality and corruption from Adam but resisting inherited **culpability**, a pattern already implicit in **Chrysostom's moralism** and in Byzantine spirituality.

The Reformation era sharpened the forensic edge. **Luther and the Formula of Concord** insisted that **original sin is total corruption** deserving wrath, precisely to magnify **sola gratia**. **Calvin's federal headship** articulated Adam as a public person within a **covenant of works**, consolidating both **imputed guilt** and **inherited corruption**. The **Westminster Confession** 1646–1648 summarized the consensus: the guilt of Adam's sin imputed, the same death in sin and corrupted nature conveyed to all descended by ordinary generation. Post-Reformation scholastics like **Francis Turretin** defended **immediate imputation**, while **Jonathan Edwards** in 1758 proposed a **divine constitution of identity**, uniting the race to Adam in a legal-moral organism.

Nineteenth-century **Old Princeton** codified the thesis through **Charles Hodge's immediate imputation** and **B. B. Warfield's federalism**, whereas **W. G. T. Shedd** combined **traducianism** with an accent on the solidarity of human nature. **Dutch Neo-Calvinism**, through **Herman Bavinck**, affirmed both **imputed guilt** and **organic corruption**, contextualizing the doctrine within **common grace and covenantal life**. Mid-twentieth-century **G. C. Berkouwer** criticized speculative excess while retaining the indispensability of the **Adam–Christ parallel**.

#### 4.**Reformed Doctrine (Old Princeton / Dutch Neo-Calvinist / Westminster)**
The **Westminster Divines** framed original sin under **covenant of works and federal headship**, teaching that Adam represented all his posterity. **Immediate imputation** asserts that the guilt of Adam's first sin is reckoned to all apart from the mediating causality of a corrupt nature, though corruption inevitably follows as penal consequence. This juridical sequence preserves the parallel with **imputed righteousness in union with Christ**, where a new head grants right standing before any moral renovation.

**Old Princeton** refined the exegesis. **Charles Hodge** argued that Romans 5 grounds **immediate imputation** in the forensic terms of condemnation and justification, while **Warfield** defended **federal theology** as the scriptural conception of solidarity. **Shedd** stressed the unity of human nature and leaned on **traducianism** to make sense of transmission, while still affirming **imputed guilt** as a divine judicial act. **Dutch Neo-Calvinists** like **Bavinck** integrated the legal with the organic, insisting that **imputed guilt and inherited corruption** are inseparable benefits and penalties of headship, and that **common grace** restrains the worst effects without negating **total depravity**. In each stream, **union with Christ** anchors the Adamic grammar, lest imputation be severed from **participation and renewal**.

#### 5.**Alternative Accounts (Roman Catholic / Lutheran / Modern / Analytic)**
**Roman Catholicism**, following **Trent 1546**, affirms that original sin is transmitted by **propagation not imitation**, and that baptism remits **reatus culpae**, the guilt of original sin, while **concupiscence remains** yet is not sin without consent. The Catholic view resists the Reformed accent on **imputed guilt as immediate**, tending to locate culpability in deprivation of original righteousness and in a new privative state. Yet the necessity of **infant baptism** witnesses to the seriousness of the inherited condition and its liability.

**Lutheran theology**, through **Augsburg Confession II** and the **Formula of Concord**, defines original sin as **total corruption deserving damnation**, while less invested in **federal headship** or the **covenant of works**. The Lutheran accent on **simul iustus et peccator** retains forensic clarity, but the debate over **mediate versus immediate imputation** is less central, since **justification by faith** does not lean on the Adam–Christ construct to the same degree.

Modern proposals vary. **Eastern Orthodoxy** develops **ancestral sin**, denying inherited guilt and emphasizing mortality and corruption as Adam's bequest, to be healed by **theosis**. **Karl Barth** rejects **imputational schemas** as externalist, reconfiguring sin as **prideful No** to God's Yes, and reading Adam as an existential possibility rather than a federal head. Evolutionary accounts raise pressure on **historical Adam**, prompting models like **genealogical Adam** that preserve **federal representation** amid common descent. In analytic theology, **Oliver Crisp** distinguishes models of **realism, federalism, and corruption-only**, pressing the metaphysics of solidarity, while **Plantinga's transworld depravity** frames universal sinfulness without imputed guilt. Yet Reformed thinkers reply that **imputed righteousness** in Christ presupposes an **imputed guilt** in Adam for the Pauline parallel to hold.

#### 6.**Current Debates and Objections**
Three objections recur. First, the fairness objection: how can God justly assign **corporate culpability** to those who did not personally consent. The Reformed answer appeals to **federal headship** as a common legal structure of representation and to the biblical pattern of **covenantal solidarity**, while noting the symmetry that God saves by **another's obedience imputed**. Second, the agency objection: if corruption is **total depravity**, how can responsibility remain. Here **Edwards's moral inability** clarifies that inability lies in the will's inclination, preserving moral accountability while magnifying the necessity of **regenerating grace**. Third, the historical objection: can **Romans 5** stand under modern science. Proposals include **genealogical Adam** within the last ten millennia, **corporate federalism** focusing on covenantal selection, and **Christological reading** that lets the clarity of the second Adam interpret the first.

Exegetically, the debate over *eph ho pantes hemarton* continues. Even if the phrase is read as "because all sinned," the subsequent verses insist that death reigned over those who did not sin like Adam, grounding death in **Adam's trespass** rather than personal acts. The stronger verbs of **constitution and condemnation** press beyond exemplarism. Analytically, philosophers revisit **collective responsibility** and **moral luck**, noting that legal systems and Scripture both recognize **representation** as morally meaningful. Pastoral concerns about infants meet the Reformed confidence that the **elect infants dying in infancy** are saved by Christ, indicating that **imputed guilt** does not negate **divine mercy**.

#### 7.**System Links (Trinity / Christology / Anthropology etc.)**
Anthropology frames the doctrine through **imago Dei wounded but not destroyed**, grounding human dignity even amid **original corruption**. Christology centers the **second Adam**, whose **active and passive obedience** are imputed to believers, restoring righteousness and inaugurating new creation. Soteriology deploys **union with Christ and justification by imputed righteousness**, mapping the Adamic pattern into **grace's federal structure**. Pneumatology explains **regeneration and sanctification**, answering **concupiscence** with indwelling holiness.

Ecclesiology and sacramentology treat **baptism** as sign and seal of incorporation into the **new head**, with Reformed churches denying automatic removal of guilt in the sign while affirming the promise signified. Eschatology completes the arc: the **resurrection of the body** in Christ overturns **Adamic death** and consummates the healing of creation's groaning. Bibliology and hermeneutics must sustain a reading of **Romans 5 and 1 Corinthians 15** that balances **historical particularity** with **theological pressure** from the gospel's grammar.

#### 8.**Pastoral and Liturgical Payoff**
Pastorally, **original sin and imputation** furnish a robust realism about the human condition. Catechesis on **original guilt and corruption** inoculates against naivete about willpower and culture, while driving hearers to **grace in Christ's imputed righteousness**. Confession of sin in Lord's Day worship rehearses **federal headship**, moving from "in Adam" to "in Christ" with assurance grounded not in inward achievement but in **alien righteousness**.

Liturgically, baptismal practice and vows place children under the **covenant headship of Christ**, teaching households that the deepest problem is not first learned behavior but **inherited condition** met by **promised grace**. Preaching the **second Adam** frames sanctification as the Spirit's outworking of a new identity. In public theology, **social and structural sins** are named without displacing personal responsibility, since **imputed guilt** and **corporate solidarity** prepare the church to confess the sins of our fathers while seeking **reconciliation in Christ**. The doctrine thus steadies counseling with honest talk of **concupiscence**, steers discipleship toward **means of grace**, and anchors assurance in a **once-for-all verdict**.

#### 9.**One concise closing sentence.**
Rightly framed, **original sin and imputation** clarify that the same federal grammar that justly implicates us in Adam gloriously exculpates us in Christ through **imputed righteousness** and **re-creating grace**.
