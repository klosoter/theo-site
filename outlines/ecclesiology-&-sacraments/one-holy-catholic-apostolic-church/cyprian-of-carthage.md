# Cyprian of Carthage (c. 200–258)

Cyprian of Carthage articulated a robust doctrine of the **one, holy, catholic, apostolic Church**, emphasizing the visible unity of the Church under the authority of the **episcopate**. He insisted that **ecclesial unity** is grounded in the **apostolic succession** and the communion of bishops, and that separation from the Church is separation from salvation. For Cyprian, the Church’s holiness and catholicity are inseparable from its unity and apostolic foundation.

- "He can no longer have God for his Father who has not the Church for his mother." (On the Unity of the Church)

## Key Works

- On the Unity of the Catholic Church (De Ecclesiae Catholicae Unitate)
- Letters (Epistulae)
- On the Lapsed (De Lapsis)
- On the Lord’s Prayer (De Oratione Dominica)
- The Oxford Handbook of Early Christian Studies (ed. Susan Ashbrook Harvey & David G. Hunter)

## Key Theological Commitments

1. **Unity of the Church is Essential**
    - The Church is fundamentally one, mirroring the unity of God.
    - Schism is a grave sin that destroys the body of Christ.
    - Unity is maintained through the communion of bishops.
    - The Church’s unity is visible and institutional, not merely spiritual.
    - "The Church is one, which with increasing fecundity extends far and wide into the multitudes." (On the Unity of the Church)

2. **Episcopacy as the Center of Unity**
    - The bishop embodies and safeguards the unity of the local church.
    - All legitimate authority in the Church flows from the episcopate.
    - Bishops collectively form a single, undivided episcopal college.
    - Apostolic succession ensures continuity with the original apostles.
    - "The episcopate is one, each part of which is held by each one for the whole." (On the Unity of the Church)

3. **No Salvation Outside the Church (Extra Ecclesiam Nulla Salus)**
    - Salvation is found only within the visible, catholic Church.
    - Those who separate from the Church forfeit access to grace.
    - Heretics and schismatics cannot possess the Holy Spirit.
    - The Church is the exclusive means of reconciliation with God.
    - "He who does not have the Church as his mother cannot have God as his Father." (On the Unity of the Church)

4. **Holiness and Catholicity are Inseparable from Unity**
    - The Church’s holiness is rooted in its unity and apostolic faith.
    - Catholicity means universality, not mere geographical spread.
    - The true Church is marked by both doctrinal purity and unity.
    - Holiness is maintained through discipline and sacramental life.
    - "The spouse of Christ cannot be adulterous; she is uncorrupted and pure." (On the Unity of the Church)

5. **Apostolicity Expressed in Succession and Teaching**
    - The Church’s authority derives from the apostles through succession.
    - Faithfulness to apostolic teaching is essential for true catholicity.
    - Bishops are guardians of apostolic doctrine and practice.
    - Apostolicity is both historical and doctrinal.
    - "We must hold fast to the tradition of the truth which comes from the Lord and through the apostles." (Letter 66)

## Legacy and Impact

Cyprian’s theology of the **one, holy, catholic, apostolic Church** profoundly shaped Western ecclesiology, especially the doctrines of **episcopal authority** and **church unity**. His insistence on the visible unity of the Church and the necessity of the episcopate influenced later debates on schism, heresy, and the nature of the Church. The maxim "no salvation outside the Church" became a foundational principle in both patristic and medieval theology. Cyprian’s thought remains a touchstone for discussions of ecclesial identity and authority in both Catholic and Protestant traditions.
