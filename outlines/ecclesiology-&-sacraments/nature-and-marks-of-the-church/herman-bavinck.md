# Herman Bavinck (1854–1921)

Herman Bavinck articulated a robust doctrine of the **Church**, emphasizing its existence as both a visible and invisible reality grounded in the **covenant of grace**. He maintained that the Church’s unity, holiness, catholicity, and apostolicity—its essential **marks**—are rooted in Christ and manifest through the preaching of the **Word**, administration of the **sacraments**, and exercise of **discipline**.

- "The church is not an institution, but a communion of saints, a people called by God, gathered around Christ, and sanctified by the Spirit." (Reformed Dogmatics, IV)

## Key Works

- Reformed Dogmatics, Vol. 4: Holy Spirit, Church, and New Creation
- The Sacrifice of Praise
- Essays on Religion, Science, and Society
- Philosophy of Revelation

## Key Theological Commitments

1. **The Church as Both Visible and Invisible**
    - The **invisible church** consists of all true believers united to Christ by faith.
    - The **visible church** is the historical, institutional community where Word and sacrament are present.
    - Both aspects are necessary for a full understanding of the Church’s nature.
    - The distinction guards against identifying the Church solely with any earthly institution.
    "The invisible church is the communion of the elect, but the visible church is the gathering of professing believers and their children." (Reformed Dogmatics, IV)

2. **The Marks of the True Church**
    - The true Church is identified by the **pure preaching of the Word**, right administration of the **sacraments**, and faithful exercise of **discipline**.
    - These marks distinguish the Church from sects and false assemblies.
    - The marks are grounded in Scripture and the Reformed confessions.
    - They serve as practical criteria for ecclesial fidelity.
    "Wherever the Word is purely preached, the sacraments are administered according to Christ’s institution, and discipline is exercised, there is the church." (Reformed Dogmatics, IV)

3. **Unity, Holiness, Catholicity, and Apostolicity**
    - The Church is **one** in Christ, transcending all divisions.
    - It is **holy**, set apart by the Spirit and called to sanctification.
    - The Church is **catholic**, embracing all times and places.
    - It is **apostolic**, rooted in the teaching and mission of the apostles.
    "The church is one, holy, catholic, and apostolic, not as an ideal, but as a reality grounded in Christ." (Reformed Dogmatics, IV)

4. **Covenantal Foundation of the Church**
    - The Church is the community of the **covenant of grace**.
    - Membership includes believers and their children, reflecting God’s covenant dealings.
    - The covenant frames the Church’s identity and mission in redemptive history.
    - This foundation ensures continuity between Old and New Testament people of God.
    "The church is the assembly of those who, with their children, are included in the covenant of grace." (Reformed Dogmatics, IV)

5. **The Church’s Mission and Eschatological Hope**
    - The Church is called to witness to Christ in the world.
    - Its mission includes worship, discipleship, and service.
    - The Church anticipates its perfection and consummation at Christ’s return.
    - Eschatological hope sustains the Church amid imperfection and struggle.
    "The church is both a pilgrim and an eschatological community, awaiting the full realization of God’s kingdom." (Reformed Dogmatics, IV)

## Legacy and Impact

Bavinck’s doctrine of the **Church** has profoundly shaped Reformed ecclesiology, especially in articulating the relationship between the visible and invisible church and the enduring **marks** of the true Church. His covenantal perspective provided a theological foundation for including children in the Church and for continuity across redemptive history. Bavinck’s balanced and nuanced approach continues to influence contemporary discussions on the Church’s identity, unity, and mission within the broader **Reformed tradition**.
