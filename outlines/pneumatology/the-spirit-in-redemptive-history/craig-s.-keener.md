# Craig S. Keener (1960– )

Craig S. Keener emphasizes the pervasive role of the **Holy Spirit** throughout **redemptive history**, highlighting the Spirit’s activity from the Old Testament through the ministry of Jesus and into the life of the early church. He stresses the continuity and expansion of **Spirit-empowerment**, **prophecy**, and **miraculous gifts** as essential to understanding the unfolding of **salvation history** in both biblical narrative and contemporary Christian experience.

- "The Spirit’s activity is not limited to the first century but is integral to the entire sweep of redemptive history." (Gift & Giver, p. 21)

## Key Works

- Gift & Giver: The Holy Spirit for Today (2001)
- Acts: An Exegetical Commentary (4 vols., 2012–2015)
- Miracles: The Credibility of the New Testament Accounts (2011)
- Spirit Hermeneutics: Reading Scripture in Light of Pentecost (2016)
- The IVP Bible Background Commentary: New Testament (1993)

## Key Theological Commitments

1. **The Spirit’s Role in Salvation History**
   - The **Holy Spirit** is active from creation through consummation, not just in isolated epochs.
   - The Spirit’s work is central to the unfolding of **God’s redemptive plan**.
   - Old Testament prophecy and promise anticipate the Spirit’s outpouring in the New Testament.
   - The Spirit empowers both individuals and communities for God’s mission.
   - "The Spirit is the agent of God’s presence and power throughout the biblical narrative." (Gift & Giver, p. 23)

2. **Continuity of Charismatic Experience**
   - The gifts and manifestations of the Spirit are not confined to the apostolic age.
   - Keener argues for the ongoing relevance of **miraculous gifts** in the church.
   - He sees the Pentecost event as paradigmatic for Christian experience.
   - The Spirit’s activity bridges the biblical world and the contemporary church.
   - "The same Spirit who empowered the early church continues to empower believers today." (Gift & Giver, p. 31)

3. **The Spirit and Mission**
   - The Spirit empowers believers for **witness** and **mission** in fulfillment of Jesus’ commission.
   - Acts is read as a model for Spirit-driven expansion of the gospel.
   - The Spirit breaks down ethnic and social barriers in the advance of God’s kingdom.
   - Mission is inseparable from the dynamic presence of the Spirit.
   - "The Spirit’s coming at Pentecost launches the church’s mission to the nations." (Acts: An Exegetical Commentary, vol. 1, p. 479)

4. **Spirit-Inspired Interpretation of Scripture**
   - The Spirit is essential for rightly understanding and applying Scripture.
   - Keener advocates a **hermeneutic** that is both scholarly and open to the Spirit’s illumination.
   - He warns against separating exegesis from spiritual experience.
   - The Spirit’s guidance is necessary for discerning the meaning and significance of biblical texts.
   - "Spirit hermeneutics means reading Scripture in light of the same Spirit who inspired it." (Spirit Hermeneutics, p. 25)

5. **Integration of Historical and Experiential Perspectives**
   - Keener combines rigorous historical scholarship with openness to contemporary experiences of the Spirit.
   - He defends the credibility of **miracles** and supernatural events in both biblical and modern contexts.
   - The Spirit’s work is both historically attested and existentially real.
   - This approach bridges academic study and lived faith.
   - "Historical evidence and present experience together testify to the Spirit’s ongoing work." (Miracles, p. 765)

## Legacy and Impact

Keener’s scholarship has significantly shaped contemporary understanding of the **Holy Spirit** in both academic and church contexts, especially regarding the continuity of **charismatic gifts** and the Spirit’s role in mission. His integration of historical-critical methods with a robust pneumatology has influenced evangelical, Pentecostal, and ecumenical discussions. Keener’s work continues to encourage a dynamic engagement with the Spirit in both biblical interpretation and Christian practice.
