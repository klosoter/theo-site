# John Murray (1898–1975)

John Murray emphasized the **integral role of the Holy Spirit** in the life, ministry, and resurrection of Christ, highlighting the Spirit’s work in the **incarnation**, **empowerment for messianic mission**, and the **vindication of Christ in resurrection**. For Murray, the Spirit’s presence is not peripheral but central to the accomplishment of **redemption**, ensuring the unity of the **Trinitarian economy** in the person and work of Christ.

- "The Holy Spirit is the agent by whom Christ was conceived, anointed for his ministry, and raised from the dead." (Redemption Accomplished and Applied)

## Key Works

- Redemption Accomplished and Applied
- Collected Writings of John Murray, Vol. 2: Systematic Theology
- The Epistle to the Romans (NICNT Commentary)
- "The Agency of the Holy Spirit in the Incarnation" (in Collected Writings)
- "The Resurrection" (in Collected Writings)

## Key Theological Commitments

1. **Spirit’s Agency in the Incarnation**
   - The Holy Spirit is the direct agent of Christ’s conception in the virgin birth.
   - The Spirit’s work ensures the true humanity and sinlessness of Christ.
   - The incarnation is a Trinitarian act, with the Spirit’s role being essential.
   - The Spirit’s agency upholds the unity of Christ’s divine and human natures.
   - "The conception of Jesus by the Holy Spirit is the guarantee of his holiness and uniqueness." (Collected Writings, Vol. 2)

2. **Spirit’s Anointing for Messianic Ministry**
   - Christ’s baptism marks the public anointing by the Spirit for his messianic office.
   - The Spirit empowers Christ’s obedience, miracles, and proclamation.
   - The Spirit’s presence fulfills Old Testament prophecy regarding the Messiah.
   - The anointing signifies Christ’s dependence on the Spirit as the second Adam.
   - "The anointing of Jesus with the Spirit at the Jordan was the authentication of his messianic commission." (Redemption Accomplished and Applied)

3. **Spirit’s Role in Christ’s Obedience and Suffering**
   - The Spirit sustains Christ in his earthly trials and temptations.
   - Christ’s obedience is rendered in the power of the Spirit.
   - The Spirit enables Christ’s self-offering as a sacrifice.
   - The Spirit’s presence is evident in Christ’s prayers and submission to the Father.
   - "It was through the eternal Spirit that he offered himself without spot to God." (Collected Writings, Vol. 2)

4. **Spirit’s Agency in the Resurrection**
   - The resurrection is attributed to the power of the Holy Spirit.
   - The Spirit vindicates Christ’s person and work in the resurrection event.
   - The resurrection inaugurates the new creation by the Spirit.
   - The Spirit’s role in resurrection is foundational for believers’ future resurrection.
   - "Christ was raised by the glory of the Father, and the Spirit is the agent of that glory." (Romans, NICNT)

5. **Spirit’s Eschatological Significance**
   - The Spirit’s work in Christ’s resurrection is the firstfruits of eschatological renewal.
   - The Spirit applies the benefits of Christ’s resurrection to believers.
   - The resurrection by the Spirit guarantees the consummation of redemption.
   - The Spirit’s role in Christ’s resurrection is paradigmatic for the church’s hope.
   - "The Spirit who raised Jesus from the dead will also give life to your mortal bodies." (Romans, NICNT)

## Legacy and Impact

Murray’s robust articulation of the **Spirit’s role in Christ’s ministry and resurrection** has shaped Reformed understanding of the **Trinitarian unity** in redemption. His careful exegesis and theological synthesis have influenced generations of systematic theologians, especially in connecting the Spirit’s work in Christ to the believer’s union with Christ. Murray’s legacy endures in contemporary discussions of **pneumatology** and the application of redemption, reinforcing the centrality of the Spirit in the economy of salvation.
