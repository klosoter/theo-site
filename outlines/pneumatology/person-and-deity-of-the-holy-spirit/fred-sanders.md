# Fred Sanders (1968–)

Fred Sanders is a contemporary evangelical theologian whose work on the **Trinity** emphasizes the full **personhood** and **deity** of the Holy Spirit within the eternal life of God. Sanders insists that the Holy Spirit is not merely a divine force but a distinct **divine person** who shares in the one **divine essence** and participates fully in the **missions** of the Trinity.

- "The Holy Spirit is not an impersonal power, but God himself, personally present and active." ("The Deep Things of God")

## Key Works

- The Deep Things of God: How the Trinity Changes Everything
- The Triune God (New Studies in Dogmatics)
- Jesus in Trinitarian Perspective (co-edited with Klaus Issler)
- "The Trinity" in The Oxford Handbook of Systematic Theology
- "The Holy Spirit: Lord and Life-Giver" (various essays and lectures)

## Key Theological Commitments

1. **The Holy Spirit is fully God**
    - Affirms the **consubstantiality** of the Spirit with the Father and the Son.
    - Rejects any subordinationist or impersonal views of the Spirit.
    - Emphasizes the Spirit’s role in both **creation** and **redemption**.
    - Grounds the Spirit’s deity in the biblical witness and historic creeds.
    - "The Spirit is not a lesser deity or a created being, but the Lord, the giver of life." (The Deep Things of God)

2. **The Holy Spirit is a distinct person**
    - Insists on the Spirit’s personal identity, not reducible to a mode or attribute.
    - Highlights the Spirit’s unique actions and relationships within the Trinity.
    - Draws on biblical language of sending, speaking, and interceding.
    - Upholds the Spirit’s personal agency in the economy of salvation.
    - "The Spirit is not just the power of God, but God as a person who empowers." (The Triune God)

3. **The Spirit’s role in the missions of the Trinity**
    - The Spirit is sent by the Father and the Son in the history of salvation.
    - The Spirit’s mission reveals his eternal relation within the Godhead.
    - The Spirit applies the work of Christ to believers and the church.
    - The Spirit’s mission is essential for the realization of God’s purposes in the world.
    - "The Spirit’s mission is the outworking of his eternal procession." (The Triune God)

4. **Biblical and creedal foundation for the Spirit’s deity**
    - Sanders grounds his doctrine in the witness of Scripture, especially the New Testament.
    - He appeals to the Nicene and Constantinopolitan Creeds for doctrinal clarity.
    - Emphasizes the continuity of orthodox trinitarianism across church history.
    - Argues that the church’s confession of the Spirit’s deity is non-negotiable.
    - "The church’s confession that the Spirit is ‘Lord and giver of life’ is rooted in Scripture and creed." (The Deep Things of God)

5. **The Spirit’s work in revelation and sanctification**
    - The Spirit inspires Scripture and illumines believers to its truth.
    - The Spirit unites believers to Christ and sanctifies them for holy living.
    - The Spirit empowers the church for mission and witness.
    - The Spirit’s presence is the guarantee of God’s indwelling and eschatological hope.
    - "The Spirit is the one who brings the reality of God’s life to bear on us, making us participants in the divine life." (The Deep Things of God)

## Legacy and Impact

Fred Sanders has significantly shaped contemporary evangelical understanding of the **Trinity**, especially regarding the **person and deity of the Holy Spirit**. His work has helped recover a robust trinitarianism that resists reductionistic or impersonal accounts of the Spirit. Sanders’s accessible yet rigorous scholarship has influenced pastors, theologians, and students, encouraging renewed attention to the Spirit’s role in Christian doctrine and life.
