# Richard B. Gaffin Jr. (1936– )

Richard B. Gaffin Jr. affirms the full **personhood** and **deity** of the **Holy Spirit** within the framework of **Reformed trinitarian theology**. He emphasizes the Spirit’s essential role in **redemptive history**, particularly in **union with Christ** and the application of **salvation**. Gaffin’s work underscores the Spirit’s distinct personhood while maintaining the Spirit’s consubstantiality and coequality with the Father and the Son.

- "The Holy Spirit is not an impersonal force but the fully divine third person of the Trinity, essential to the accomplishment and application of redemption." (Perspectives on Pentecost)

## Key Works

- Perspectives on Pentecost: New Testament Teaching on the Gifts of the Holy Spirit
- By Faith, Not by Sight: Paul and the Order of Salvation
- Resurrection and Redemption: A Study in Paul’s Soteriology
- "The Holy Spirit" in The New Dictionary of Theology (ed. Sinclair Ferguson, David Wright, J.I. Packer)
- Articles in Westminster Theological Journal on pneumatology and trinitarian theology

## Key Theological Commitments

1. **The Holy Spirit as Fully Divine and Personal**
    - Affirms the Spirit’s full deity, coequal with the Father and the Son.
    - Rejects any subordinationist or impersonal views of the Spirit.
    - Emphasizes the Spirit’s distinct personal identity within the Godhead.
    - Grounds the Spirit’s personhood in biblical revelation and historic creeds.
    "The Spirit is not merely a divine influence but a distinct person who is truly God." (Perspectives on Pentecost)

2. **The Spirit’s Role in Redemptive History**
    - The Spirit is central to the accomplishment and application of redemption.
    - Highlights the Spirit’s work in Christ’s resurrection and Pentecost.
    - Sees the Spirit as the eschatological gift promised in the Old Testament.
    - Connects the Spirit’s work to the unfolding of God’s redemptive plan.
    "The outpouring of the Spirit at Pentecost marks the beginning of the eschatological age." (Perspectives on Pentecost)

3. **Union with Christ by the Spirit**
    - The Spirit unites believers to Christ, making his benefits effective.
    - Union with Christ is the foundation of all soteriological blessings.
    - The Spirit’s indwelling is essential for participation in Christ’s resurrection life.
    - Emphasizes the Spirit’s role in both justification and sanctification.
    "Union with Christ by the Spirit is at the heart of Paul’s soteriology." (By Faith, Not by Sight)

4. **The Spirit and the Word**
    - The Spirit is the divine agent of inspiration and illumination.
    - Scripture is the Spirit’s instrument for revealing Christ and applying salvation.
    - The Spirit never works apart from the Word in the life of the church.
    - Emphasizes the inseparability of Word and Spirit in Reformed theology.
    "The Spirit and the Word are inseparable in the economy of salvation." (Perspectives on Pentecost)

5. **The Spirit’s Eschatological Ministry**
    - The Spirit inaugurates the new creation in the resurrection of Christ.
    - Believers experience the “firstfruits” of the Spirit as a foretaste of the age to come.
    - The Spirit’s presence is both a guarantee and anticipation of final redemption.
    - Eschatology is not merely future but is realized in the Spirit’s present work.
    "The Spirit is the eschatological gift, the down payment of the inheritance to come." (Resurrection and Redemption)

## Legacy and Impact

Gaffin’s robust articulation of the **person and deity of the Holy Spirit** has significantly shaped contemporary Reformed **pneumatology**. His integration of the Spirit’s personhood with redemptive history and union with Christ has influenced both academic theology and pastoral ministry. Gaffin’s work remains a key resource for understanding the Spirit’s role in the **Trinity** and the application of salvation within the Reformed tradition.
