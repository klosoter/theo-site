# Geerhardus Vos (1862–1949)

Geerhardus Vos understood **regeneration** as a sovereign act of the **Holy Spirit** that imparts new spiritual life, forming the foundation for all subsequent aspects of **salvation**. He emphasized the **indwelling of the Spirit** as the means by which believers are united to **Christ** and enabled to live in obedience. Vos’s biblical-theological approach highlighted the eschatological character of these doctrines, seeing them as central to the unfolding of **redemptive history**.

- "Regeneration is the implanting of the principle of the new life, and the indwelling Spirit is the bond of union with Christ." (Reformed Dogmatics, Vol. 4)

## Key Works

- Reformed Dogmatics, Vol. 4: Soteriology
- The Pauline Eschatology
- Biblical Theology: Old and New Testaments
- "The Scriptural Doctrine of the Love of God" (Princeton Theological Review)
- "The Eschatological Aspect of the Pauline Conception of the Spirit" (Biblical and Theological Studies)

## Key Theological Commitments

1. **Regeneration as Monergistic Work of the Spirit**
    - Regeneration is solely the work of the **Holy Spirit**, not a cooperative act between God and man.
    - It is the initial act in the **ordo salutis**, preceding faith and repentance.
    - This act implants a new principle of spiritual life in the elect.
    - Human will is passive in regeneration, being acted upon by divine power.
    - "Regeneration is not a change effected in cooperation with the human will, but a creative act of God." (Reformed Dogmatics, Vol. 4)

2. **Indwelling of the Spirit as Union with Christ**
    - The **indwelling Spirit** is the bond that unites believers to **Christ**.
    - This union is both mystical and vital, enabling participation in Christ’s benefits.
    - The indwelling is permanent and marks the believer as belonging to the new creation.
    - It is through the Spirit’s indwelling that sanctification and perseverance are effected.
    - "The indwelling of the Spirit is the means by which the believer is organically united to Christ." (Reformed Dogmatics, Vol. 4)

3. **Eschatological Character of Regeneration and Indwelling**
    - Regeneration inaugurates participation in the **eschatological age**.
    - The Spirit’s indwelling is the firstfruits of the final redemption.
    - Vos connects individual renewal to the broader cosmic renewal in redemptive history.
    - The believer’s new life is a present experience of the age to come.
    - "The Spirit is the earnest of the inheritance, the pledge of the eschatological consummation." (The Pauline Eschatology)

4. **Biblical-Theological Method in Soteriology**
    - Vos interprets regeneration and indwelling within the unfolding of **redemptive history**.
    - He stresses the continuity and development of these doctrines from Old to New Testament.
    - The Spirit’s work is seen as climactic in the New Covenant era.
    - This method highlights the organic unity of Scripture’s teaching on salvation.
    - "Biblical theology seeks to trace the historical process of revelation, in which regeneration and indwelling are central themes." (Biblical Theology)

5. **Objective and Subjective Aspects of Salvation**
    - Regeneration is the objective act of God imparting life; indwelling is the subjective experience of that life.
    - Both are necessary for the believer’s assurance and growth in holiness.
    - The Spirit’s presence assures believers of their adoption and inheritance.
    - Vos maintains the inseparability of forensic and transformative aspects of salvation.
    - "The subjective realization of salvation is inseparably connected with the objective accomplishment in Christ." (Reformed Dogmatics, Vol. 4)

## Legacy and Impact

Vos’s robust articulation of **regeneration** and the **indwelling Spirit** has profoundly shaped Reformed soteriology, especially in emphasizing the eschatological and redemptive-historical dimensions. His biblical-theological method provided a framework for understanding these doctrines as central to the believer’s union with Christ and participation in the new creation. Vos’s influence endures in contemporary Reformed theology, particularly in discussions of the Holy Spirit’s role in salvation and the unfolding of redemptive history.
