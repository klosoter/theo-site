# Vern Poythress (1946–)

Vern Poythress approaches **sanctification** through the lens of **biblical theology** and **multiperspectivalism**, emphasizing both its **definitive** and **progressive** dimensions. He affirms that believers are set apart in Christ once-for-all, while also being called to ongoing transformation by the **Holy Spirit** in union with Christ. Poythress integrates **Reformed soteriology** with a strong focus on the practical outworking of sanctification in the life of the church.

- "Sanctification is both a definitive act of God and a continuing process in the life of the believer." (Symphonic Theology)

- The Shadow of Christ in the Law of Moses

## Key Theological Commitments

1. **Definitive Sanctification in Union with Christ**
    - Believers are decisively set apart by God at conversion.
    - This sanctification is rooted in the believer’s union with Christ’s death and resurrection.
    - It is a once-for-all status change, not dependent on human effort.
    - Definitive sanctification provides the foundation for all subsequent growth.
    - "Definitive sanctification is grounded in our participation in Christ’s finished work." (Symphonic Theology)

2. **Progressive Sanctification as Spirit-Empowered Transformation**
    - Sanctification continues throughout the believer’s life as a process.
    - The Holy Spirit enables ongoing growth in holiness and obedience.
    - Progressive sanctification involves both divine initiative and human responsibility.
    - It is marked by increasing conformity to the image of Christ.
    - "The Spirit works in us to bring about the continuing transformation that marks Christian maturity." (Symphonic Theology)

3. **Multiperspectival Approach to Sanctification**
    - Sanctification can be understood from multiple biblical perspectives (normative, situational, existential).
    - Each perspective highlights different aspects: God’s commands, life circumstances, and personal experience.
    - Multiperspectivalism guards against reductionism in understanding sanctification.
    - This approach encourages holistic discipleship and application.
    - "We need to see sanctification from the perspectives of God’s law, the work of Christ, and the indwelling Spirit." (Symphonic Theology)

4. **Integration of Law and Gospel in Sanctification**
    - The law reveals God’s standard and exposes sin, driving believers to Christ.
    - The gospel provides the power and motivation for sanctification.
    - Sanctification is not achieved by law-keeping but is the fruit of grace.
    - The law continues to guide believers as they grow in Christlikeness.
    - "Law and gospel together shape the process of sanctification in the Christian life." (The Shadow of Christ in the Law of Moses)

5. **Corporate and Ecclesial Dimensions of Sanctification**
    - Sanctification is not merely individual but occurs within the context of the church.
    - The means of grace (Word, sacraments, prayer) are central to communal growth.
    - Mutual encouragement and accountability foster progressive sanctification.
    - The church’s holiness is both a witness and a foretaste of the coming kingdom.
    - "Sanctification is nurtured in the community of faith as we build one another up in Christ." (Symphonic Theology)

## Legacy and Impact

Poythress’s articulation of **definitive and progressive sanctification** has enriched contemporary Reformed theology by emphasizing both the objective and experiential aspects of salvation. His **multiperspectival** method has encouraged theologians and pastors to approach sanctification holistically, integrating doctrinal, practical, and communal dimensions. Poythress’s work continues to influence discussions on the relationship between law and gospel, and the role of the church in spiritual growth.
