# Richard B. Gaffin Jr. (1936–)

Richard B. Gaffin Jr. is a Reformed theologian whose engagement with the **Chalcedonian Definition** and the **two-natures doctrine** centers on the integrity of Christ’s **true humanity** and **undiminished deity**. Gaffin emphasizes the enduring significance of **Chalcedonian Christology** for maintaining the unity of Christ’s person while upholding the distinction of his **divine and human natures**, particularly in relation to **redemptive history** and the **work of the Holy Spirit**.

- "The Chalcedonian formula remains the indispensable framework for a biblically faithful Christology." (Resurrection and Redemption)

## Key Works

- Resurrection and Redemption: A Study in Paul’s Soteriology
- "The Incarnation" in The New Dictionary of Theology
- By Faith, Not by Sight: Paul and the Order of Salvation
- "Biblical Theology and the Westminster Standards" (Westminster Theological Journal)
- Calvin and the Sabbath: The Controversy of Applying the Fourth Commandment

## Key Theological Commitments

1. **Affirmation of the Chalcedonian Definition**
    - Upholds the **Chalcedonian formula** as the essential articulation of Christ’s person.
    - Stresses the necessity of confessing Christ as **one person in two natures**.
    - Argues that Chalcedon provides the proper boundaries against both **Nestorianism** and **Eutychianism**.
    - Sees Chalcedon as foundational for orthodox **Reformed Christology**.
    - "Chalcedon’s boundaries are not to be transgressed if we are to remain within the parameters of biblical Christology." (Resurrection and Redemption)

2. **Unity of the Person of Christ**
    - Emphasizes the **hypostatic union** as the union of two natures in the one person of the Son.
    - Rejects any division or confusion between the **divine** and **human** in Christ.
    - Maintains that all of Christ’s actions are theandric, performed by the one person.
    - Insists that the unity of Christ’s person grounds the efficacy of redemption.
    - "The unity of Christ’s person is the sine qua non of his mediatorial work." (By Faith, Not by Sight)

3. **Full Reality of Christ’s Humanity**
    - Asserts the **true and complete humanity** of Christ, including a human mind, will, and body.
    - Opposes any docetic or Apollinarian tendencies that diminish Christ’s humanity.
    - Connects Christ’s humanity to his representative role as the last Adam.
    - Highlights the ongoing significance of Christ’s humanity in his resurrection and exaltation.
    - "The resurrection of Christ is the resurrection of the truly human Son of God." (Resurrection and Redemption)

4. **Continuity of Christ’s Two Natures in Redemptive History**
    - Views the two-natures doctrine as essential for understanding Christ’s work in history.
    - Argues that the **incarnation** is not a temporary event but an abiding reality.
    - Relates the two natures to Christ’s ongoing mediatorial ministry.
    - Emphasizes the Spirit’s role in the incarnate life and ministry of Christ.
    - "The incarnate Son remains forever the God-man, the mediator between God and men." (The Incarnation, New Dictionary of Theology)

5. **Redemptive-Historical Focus**
    - Integrates Chalcedonian Christology with the unfolding of **redemptive history**.
    - Sees Christ’s person and work as the center of the history of salvation.
    - Argues that the two-natures doctrine safeguards the reality of Christ’s saving work.
    - Insists that the resurrection vindicates both the deity and humanity of Christ.
    - "The resurrection is the climactic manifestation of the unity and distinction of Christ’s two natures." (Resurrection and Redemption)

## Legacy and Impact

Gaffin’s robust articulation of the **Chalcedonian Definition** has shaped contemporary Reformed engagement with Christology, especially in relation to **redemptive history** and the application of Christ’s work to the believer. His insistence on the enduring relevance of the **two-natures doctrine** has provided a bulwark against both ancient and modern Christological errors. Gaffin’s work continues to influence pastors, scholars, and students committed to confessional orthodoxy and biblical fidelity.
