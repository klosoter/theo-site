# Abraham Kuyper (1837–1920)

Abraham Kuyper articulated a robust doctrine of **divine sovereignty** that grounds both **divine foreknowledge** and **providence** in God's absolute rule over all spheres of life. He affirmed that God's governance extends even over the existence of **evil**, which is permitted for the manifestation of God's glory and the unfolding of His redemptive purposes. Kuyper’s approach insists that human responsibility and the reality of evil are compatible with God’s meticulous providence.

- "There is not a square inch in the whole domain of our human existence over which Christ, who is Sovereign over all, does not cry: Mine!" (Lectures on Calvinism)

## Key Works

- Lectures on Calvinism (1898)
- Encyclopedia of Sacred Theology (1898)
- Principles of Sacred Theology (1898)
- Common Grace (De Gemeene Gratie, 1902–1905)
- Pro Rege (1911–1928)

## Key Theological Commitments

1. **Absolute Divine Sovereignty**
    - God’s sovereignty encompasses all creation, history, and human affairs.
    - Nothing occurs outside of God’s **decretive will**, including evil.
    - Divine foreknowledge is grounded in God’s eternal decree.
    - Human actions are secondary causes under God’s primary causality.
    - "God’s sovereignty is not only the highest but also the only rule of life." (Lectures on Calvinism)

2. **Providence as Preservation, Cooperation, and Government**
    - Providence includes God’s ongoing preservation of creation.
    - God cooperates with all created things, enabling their actions.
    - God governs all events to fulfill His purposes.
    - Providence is comprehensive, extending to both good and evil acts.
    - "Providence is nothing else than the continued action of that omnipotence by which all things exist." (Encyclopedia of Sacred Theology)

3. **Divine Foreknowledge Rooted in Decree**
    - God’s knowledge of the future is exhaustive and certain.
    - Foreknowledge is not passive observation but active ordination.
    - God’s decree establishes the certainty of all events.
    - Human freedom operates within the bounds of God’s foreordained plan.
    - "God knows all things because He has decreed all things." (Principles of Sacred Theology)

4. **The Problem of Evil and the Display of God’s Glory**
    - Evil exists by God’s permission, not by His direct causation.
    - The existence of evil serves the greater purpose of manifesting God’s justice and mercy.
    - God overrules evil for the ultimate good of His creation.
    - The mystery of evil’s origin remains, but its end is certain in God’s redemptive plan.
    - "Evil is not an independent power, but is comprehended in the counsel of God." (Common Grace)

5. **Common Grace as Restraint and Preparation**
    - Common grace restrains the full effects of evil in the world.
    - It provides a context for human culture and societal development.
    - Common grace prepares the way for **special grace** and redemption.
    - God’s providence in common grace demonstrates His goodness to all humanity.
    - "Common grace explains how the world continues despite the presence of sin." (Common Grace)

## Legacy and Impact

Kuyper’s theology of **providence** and **divine sovereignty** has profoundly shaped Reformed thought, especially in the Neo-Calvinist tradition. His articulation of **common grace** provided a framework for engaging culture while upholding the seriousness of the problem of evil. Kuyper’s influence endures in discussions of the relationship between God’s sovereignty, human responsibility, and the presence of evil in the world. His legacy continues to inform contemporary debates on providence and theodicy within Reformed and broader Christian theology.
