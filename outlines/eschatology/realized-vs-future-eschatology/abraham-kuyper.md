# Abraham Kuyper (1837–1920)

Abraham Kuyper approached **eschatology** with a strong emphasis on the **already/not yet** dynamic, affirming that the **kingdom of God** is both a present reality and a future hope. He stressed the **sovereignty of Christ** over all spheres, arguing that the **renewal of creation** begins now but awaits consummation at Christ’s return. Kuyper’s vision integrates **realized eschatology** with a robust expectation of the **future consummation**.

- "The kingdom of God is present wherever Christ rules, but its full glory is yet to be revealed." (Lectures on Calvinism)

## Key Works

- Lectures on Calvinism
- Pro Rege (3 vols.)
- The Work of the Holy Spirit
- Our Worship
- Encyclopedia of Sacred Theology

## Key Theological Commitments

1. **The Kingdom is Both Present and Future**
    - The **kingdom of God** is inaugurated in Christ’s first coming but awaits final fulfillment.
    - Believers participate in the kingdom now through faith and obedience.
    - The present age is marked by both victory and struggle against sin.
    - The full realization of the kingdom will occur at Christ’s return.
    - "The kingdom is a present reality, but its perfection lies ahead." (Pro Rege)

2. **Cosmic Scope of Redemption**
    - Redemption affects not only individuals but all of creation.
    - The **renewal of creation** is underway but not yet complete.
    - Every sphere of life is to be brought under Christ’s lordship.
    - The future will see the restoration of the entire cosmos.
    - "Grace restores nature, and this restoration is both now and not yet." (Lectures on Calvinism)

3. **Antithesis and Common Grace**
    - The present age is characterized by the antithesis between belief and unbelief.
    - **Common grace** restrains evil and allows cultural development before the end.
    - Realized eschatology is seen in the ongoing conflict and transformation.
    - The final separation will occur at the consummation.
    - "Common grace prepares the world for the final revelation of Christ’s reign." (Common Grace)

4. **Christ’s Sovereignty Over All Spheres**
    - Christ’s reign is not limited to the church but extends to all of life.
    - The **already/not yet** is evident in the gradual subjection of all things to Christ.
    - Christians are called to manifest Christ’s rule in every area now.
    - The future will bring the visible, universal acknowledgment of Christ’s sovereignty.
    - "There is not a square inch in the whole domain of our human existence over which Christ... does not cry, 'Mine!'" (Sphere Sovereignty address)

5. **Hope in the Consummation**
    - The present experience of the kingdom is marked by hope and longing.
    - Suffering and imperfection persist until the final consummation.
    - The church lives in the tension of the **already/not yet**.
    - Ultimate fulfillment and restoration are guaranteed by Christ’s victory.
    - "Our hope is anchored in the certainty of Christ’s return and the renewal of all things." (Pro Rege)

## Legacy and Impact

Kuyper’s integration of **realized and future eschatology** profoundly shaped Reformed thought, especially in the areas of **public theology** and **cultural engagement**. His emphasis on the **sovereignty of Christ** over all creation continues to influence neo-Calvinist approaches to society and politics. Kuyper’s vision encourages Christians to work for renewal now while maintaining hope in the ultimate consummation of God’s kingdom.
