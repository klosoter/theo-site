# Cornelius Van Til (1895–1987)

Cornelius Van Til approached the **Covenant of Redemption** as an essential aspect of the **Trinitarian foundation** of Reformed theology. He emphasized the **intra-Trinitarian agreement** between the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit as the eternal basis for the **Covenant of Grace**, highlighting the unity of God's plan and the necessity of a distinctly **Reformed doctrine of God** for understanding redemption.

- "The covenant of redemption is the eternal agreement within the Godhead by which the Father gave the Son as head and redeemer of the elect." (The Defense of the Faith)

## Key Works

- The Defense of the Faith
- Introduction to Systematic Theology
- Christian Theistic Ethics
- Reformed Dogmatics (lecture notes)
- Essays on Christian Education

## Key Theological Commitments

1. **Covenant of Redemption as Intra-Trinitarian Pact**
   - The **Covenant of Redemption** is an eternal agreement among the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit.
   - It establishes the Son as the representative and redeemer of the elect.
   - This covenant is foundational for the **Covenant of Grace** in history.
   - It reflects the unity and diversity within the **Trinity**.
   - "The covenant of redemption is the pre-temporal ground of the historical covenant of grace." (Introduction to Systematic Theology)

2. **Absolute Sovereignty of God in Redemption**
   - God's sovereignty is expressed in the eternal counsel of the **Trinity**.
   - The plan of redemption is not contingent on human action.
   - The **Covenant of Redemption** ensures the certainty of salvation for the elect.
   - All redemptive history unfolds according to God's immutable will.
   - "God’s counsel is the presupposition of all history, including the history of redemption." (The Defense of the Faith)

3. **Unity of Ontology and Economy**
   - The ontological Trinity is the basis for the economic outworking of redemption.
   - The **Covenant of Redemption** bridges the eternal being of God and His actions in history.
   - There is no division or subordinationism within the Godhead in this covenant.
   - The economic roles of Father, Son, and Spirit reflect their eternal relations.
   - "The persons of the Trinity work in complete harmony, both in being and in action." (Introduction to Systematic Theology)

4. **Rejection of Rationalistic or Non-Reformed Approaches**
   - Van Til critiques non-Reformed views that separate God's decree from His covenantal dealings.
   - He opposes any conception of the **Covenant of Redemption** that undermines the unity of the Godhead.
   - The doctrine must be grounded in **Scripture** and the Reformed confessions.
   - Rationalistic distortions lead to a loss of the covenantal structure of revelation.
   - "Only the Reformed doctrine maintains the full biblical view of the covenant as rooted in the Trinity." (Christian Theistic Ethics)

5. **Covenant as the Framework for Apologetics**
   - The **Covenant of Redemption** undergirds Van Til’s presuppositional apologetics.
   - The certainty of redemption provides the foundation for Christian epistemology.
   - The covenantal relationship defines the Creator-creature distinction.
   - Apologetic method must reflect the covenantal structure of revelation and redemption.
   - "Our defense of the faith must rest upon the covenantal plan established in eternity." (The Defense of the Faith)

## Legacy and Impact

Van Til’s articulation of the **Covenant of Redemption** reinforced the centrality of the **Trinity** and the unity of God’s redemptive purpose in Reformed theology. His approach shaped generations of theologians and apologists, especially within the Westminster tradition, by insisting that covenant theology is inseparable from a robust doctrine of God. The covenantal framework he championed remains influential in discussions of both systematic theology and Reformed apologetics.
