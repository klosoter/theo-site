# Patrick Gillespie (1617–1675)

Patrick Gillespie was a Scottish theologian who articulated a robust doctrine of the **Covenant of Redemption** as the eternal, intra-Trinitarian agreement that grounds the **Covenant of Grace**. He emphasized the distinct roles of the **Father** and **Son** in this pact, highlighting the Son’s voluntary undertaking of redemption and the Father’s promise of reward. Gillespie’s formulation underscores the unity of the divine will and the certainty of salvation for the elect.

- "The Covenant of Redemption is the foundation of all the gracious transactions betwixt God and man." (The Ark of the Testament Opened)

## Key Works

- The Ark of the Testament Opened (1677)
- The Ark of the Covenant Opened, or, A Treatise of the Covenant of Redemption (1677)
- Select Scottish Presbyterian Sermons (various editions)
- John Coffey, Politics, Religion and the British Revolutions: The Mind of Samuel Rutherford (for context on Gillespie’s milieu)

## Key Theological Commitments

1. **Eternal Intra-Trinitarian Pact**
    - The **Covenant of Redemption** is an eternal agreement within the Godhead, especially between the **Father** and the **Son**.
    - It precedes and grounds the **Covenant of Grace** made with humanity.
    - The pact is not merely figurative but a real, binding arrangement.
    - It demonstrates the unity and harmony of the divine will in redemption.
    "This Covenant was made from eternity betwixt the Father and the Son, before the world began." (The Ark of the Testament Opened)

2. **Distinct Roles of Father and Son**
    - The **Father** appoints the Son as Mediator and assigns Him the work of redemption.
    - The **Son** voluntarily accepts the mission, undertaking to fulfill all righteousness for the elect.
    - The Father promises to equip, sustain, and reward the Son for His obedience.
    - The Son’s obedience is both active and passive, encompassing His whole mediatorial work.
    "The Father doth promise to furnish the Son with all necessary furniture for the work, and to reward him for his service." (The Ark of the Testament Opened)

3. **Foundation of the Covenant of Grace**
    - The **Covenant of Redemption** is the legal and theological basis for the **Covenant of Grace**.
    - All the blessings of salvation flow from this eternal agreement.
    - The elect’s salvation is secured by the Son’s fulfillment of the covenant terms.
    - The certainty and efficacy of redemption rest on the unbreakable pact within the Trinity.
    "All the gracious transactions betwixt God and man are founded upon this Covenant." (The Ark of the Testament Opened)

4. **Particularity and Efficacy**
    - The covenant is made specifically for the elect, not universally for all humanity.
    - Christ’s work is effectual and guarantees the salvation of those given to Him by the Father.
    - The number and identity of the elect are determined in this eternal counsel.
    - The atonement’s efficacy is rooted in the covenant’s particularity.
    "The persons for whom Christ undertook are particularly designed and given to him by the Father." (The Ark of the Testament Opened)

5. **Christ’s Suretyship and Obedience**
    - Christ acts as the **Surety** and representative for the elect in the covenant.
    - His obedience and satisfaction are accepted on their behalf.
    - The Son’s mediatorial work is both substitutionary and vicarious.
    - The Father’s acceptance of Christ’s work secures the benefits for the elect.
    "Christ became Surety for the elect, undertaking all that was required for their redemption." (The Ark of the Testament Opened)

## Legacy and Impact

Gillespie’s doctrine of the **Covenant of Redemption** became a cornerstone in later **Reformed federal theology**, influencing both Scottish and continental theologians. His clear articulation of the eternal pact within the Trinity provided a systematic foundation for understanding the certainty and particularity of redemption. Gillespie’s work contributed to the confessional formulations of the **Westminster Standards** and shaped subsequent discussions on the relationship between the **Covenant of Redemption** and the **Covenant of Grace**. His legacy endures in the ongoing Reformed emphasis on the unity and efficacy of God’s redemptive plan.
