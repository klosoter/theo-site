# B.B. Warfield (1851–1921)

B.B. Warfield did not directly formulate **Klinean covenant theology** or the concept of **intrusion ethics**, but his robust articulation of **Reformed covenantal structure** and his defense of **biblical authority** laid essential groundwork for later developments in these areas. Warfield emphasized the unity and progression of **redemptive history**, focusing on the continuity of the **covenants** and the unfolding of **divine revelation** in Scripture.

- "The Bible is the record of the successive steps by which God has, in time, wrought out His eternal plan of salvation for man." (Revelation and Inspiration)

## Key Works

- The Plan of Salvation
- Revelation and Inspiration
- Biblical and Theological Studies (collected essays)
- The Westminster Assembly and Its Work
- The Inspiration and Authority of the Bible

## Key Theological Commitments

1. **Covenant as the Structure of Redemptive History**
    - Warfield viewed the **covenant** as the organizing principle of God’s dealings with humanity.
    - He emphasized the unity of the **covenant of grace** throughout Scripture.
    - Saw the **covenant of works** as foundational for understanding the fall and redemption.
    - Understood the covenants as progressive revelations of God’s redemptive purpose.
    "The covenant of grace is but the execution in time of the eternal purpose of God." (The Plan of Salvation)

2. **Organic Unity of Scripture**
    - Scripture reveals a single, unfolding **redemptive narrative**.
    - Warfield rejected sharp discontinuities between Old and New Testaments.
    - He argued that all Scripture points to Christ and the fulfillment of the **covenant promises**.
    - Emphasized the coherence of biblical revelation as divinely orchestrated.
    "The Old Testament is the preparation for Christ; the New Testament is the presentation of Christ." (Revelation and Inspiration)

3. **Divine Sovereignty and Human Responsibility**
    - Warfield maintained the **sovereignty of God** in all covenantal arrangements.
    - He affirmed human responsibility within the framework of divine initiative.
    - Saw the law as expressing God’s righteous standard, highlighting human need for grace.
    - Understood the covenants as both gracious and demanding obedience.
    "God’s sovereignty and man’s responsibility are not antagonists, but allies." (The Plan of Salvation)

4. **Typology and Foreshadowing in Redemptive History**
    - Warfield recognized the presence of **types and shadows** in the Old Testament.
    - He saw these as preparatory, pointing forward to the reality in Christ.
    - Emphasized the pedagogical function of Old Testament law and narrative.
    - Did not explicitly articulate “intrusion ethics,” but acknowledged provisional aspects of Mosaic law.
    "The Old Testament is full of prophecy, type, and preparation." (Revelation and Inspiration)

5. **Authority and Inspiration of Scripture**
    - Warfield was a leading defender of **biblical inerrancy** and **inspiration**.
    - He insisted that all covenantal theology must be grounded in the authority of Scripture.
    - Saw the Bible as the infallible record of God’s redemptive covenants.
    - Upheld the necessity of exegesis for understanding covenantal development.
    "The Scriptures are the Word of God in such a sense that whatever they say, God says." (The Inspiration and Authority of the Bible)

## Legacy and Impact

Warfield’s rigorous defense of **covenant theology** and the unity of Scripture provided a foundation for later Reformed theologians, including those who developed **Klinean covenant theology** and **intrusion ethics**. While he did not explicitly formulate these later concepts, his work on the **organic unity of redemptive history** and the centrality of the covenants influenced the trajectory of twentieth-century Reformed thought. Warfield’s legacy endures in the ongoing commitment to **biblical authority** and covenantal interpretation within the Reformed tradition.
