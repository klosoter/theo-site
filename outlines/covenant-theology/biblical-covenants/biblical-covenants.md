### TITLE
One promise through many forms: the unity and distinction of the **covenant of grace** and **covenant of works** in Scripture and dogma

### PREVIEW NOTES
- Time and dogmatic location: from **biblical covenants** in Genesis through **Westminster federal theology** in 1646–1648 to modern revisions after 1960; locus under Theology Proper and Soteriology with links to **Ecclesiology** and **Eschatology**.
- Core biblical or theological question: how **divine promise and law** structure redemptive history and ground **federal headship** and sacramental life.
- Principal figures or schools: **Cocceius** as federal architect, **Witsius** harmonizer, **Hodge and Vos** at Old Princeton, **Kuyper and Bavinck** in Dutch Neo-Calvinism, **Kline** on treaty form; alternatives from **Barth** to **Dispensationalism**.
- Key concepts or loci connections: **pactum salutis**, **covenant of works**, **covenant of grace**, **new covenant**, **typology** and **law-gospel distinction**.
- Major texts, events, or works with dates: Genesis 15 and 17, Exodus 19–24, 2 Samuel 7, Jeremiah 31, Luke 22, Hebrews 8–10; **Westminster Confession 7** in 1646–1647; **Witsius 1677** Economy of the Covenants; **Kline 1968** Treaty of the Great King.
- Reformed and alternative trajectories: **Westminster bicovenantalism** with intratrinitarian **pactum salutis** versus **Lutheran law-gospel**, **Roman Catholic sacramental covenantalism**, **Dispensationalism** and **progressive covenantalism**.
- Legacy cue or continuing debate: **republication at Sinai**, **merit and grace** in Adam, **New Perspective on Paul**, and **baptismal administration** within the new covenant.

### ESSAY 1500-1800 words.
#### 1.**Definition & Dogmatic Location**
At its core, a biblical covenant is a divinely administered bond in blood, sovereignly bestowed, that binds God to a people through promise, obligation, and oath, a definition distilled from **meredith klines treaty model** and classical **federal theology**. The Reformed tradition distinguishes a **covenant of works** with Adam and a **covenant of grace** inaugurated after the fall, while locating an eternal **pactum salutis** among the divine persons as the Trinitys decree of redemption. This triadic pattern serves Theology Proper by articulating **divine condescension**, grounds Anthropology through Adamic **federal headship**, and orders Soteriology by mapping promise and law across history.

Dogmatically, covenants are the historical form of the one decree of God, so they function as the economy of revelation and salvation rather than as the essence of God, which is protected by **divine simplicity** and the immutability of the decree. Ecclesiology is shaped by covenants through the formation of a visible people marked by **sacramental signs**, while Eschatology reads the new covenant as the climactic administration of the **covenant of grace**. The whole doctrine mediates the law-gospel relation by placing conditions within covenants without collapsing the freeness of **justification by faith**.

#### 2.**Biblical Foundations**
The Old Testament terms *berit* and oath language establish the structural prominence of covenant in Genesis through Kings and the Prophets, while the New Testament term *diatheke* reframes covenant under Christ. Genesis 1–3 raises Adamic vocation, and Hosea 6:7 likely implies a **primeval covenant** broken by Adam, which supports a **covenant of works** defined by probation and life upon obedience. The Noahic stabilization in Genesis 9 as a **common grace covenant** secures history for redemption, while the Abrahamic covenant in Genesis 15 and 17 unites promise and sign under **imputed righteousness** through faith.

Exodus 19–24 presents a Sinai covenant in the form of ancient Near Eastern suzerainty treaties, a pattern highlighted by **mendenhall and kline** to show law within a gracious historical preface, the exodus redemption. Deuteronomy 28–30 adds covenant curses and blessings that frame Israel as typological son under a **works principle** for land tenure without undoing the deeper **covenant of grace**. The Davidic covenant in 2 Samuel 7 promises an eternal throne, and Psalm 89 reiterates oath language, all anticipating a messianic **mediator-king** who will bear covenant sanctions.

Jeremiah 31 promises a new covenant with internalized law and definitive forgiveness, while Ezekiel 36–37 speaks of Spirit-wrought renewal, all of which the New Testament applies to the inauguration of **new covenant** realities in Christ. Luke 22:20 and 1 Corinthians 11 anchor the Supper as the sign of the **new covenant in Christ’s blood**, Hebrews 8–10 argues for the obsolescence of the old administration, and Galatians 3–4 reads Abraham as the matrix of **justification apart from the law**. Romans 5 sets Adam and Christ as heads in **federal representation**, which is the backbone of covenant soteriology.

#### 3.**Historical Development**
Patristic writers such as **Irenaeus** and **Augustine** recognized an Adam-Christ parallel and a universal economy of God, though they did not deploy a full **federal schema**. Augustine’s two cities sowed seeds for a distinction between common and redemptive orders and treated sacraments as visible words, laying foundations for **covenantal signs**. Medieval authors like **Aquinas** described the New Law as grace poured into the heart, which would later shape **Roman Catholic covenantal ethics** that bind law and charity.

The Reformation sharpened the law-gospel distinction without dissolving covenant continuity. **Bullinger’s De Testamento 1534** stressed unity of the one testament, while **Calvin’s Institutes 1559** expounded one **covenant of grace** across the ages with divers administrations. In the seventeenth century, **Ursinus and Olevianus** framed the Heidelberg Catechism covenantally, **Cocceius 1648** developed a historical-covenantal progression, and **Witsius 1677** systematized the **covenant of works**, **covenant of grace**, and **pactum salutis** into a lasting synthesis.

Post-Reformation trajectories matured in two streams. British federalism crystallized at the **Westminster Assembly 1646–1648**, especially Confession 7 on **God’s covenant with man**, while Dutch federalism integrated emerging historical consciousness and **redemptive-historical exegesis**. By the nineteenth century, **Hodge and Warfield** at Old Princeton guarded bicovenantal orthodoxy against moralism, and in the twentieth century **Vos 1930** and **Kline 1968** recast covenant in biblical-theological and treaty-form terms, renewing **federal headship** while refining law in Mosaic administration.

#### 4.**Reformed Doctrine (Old Princeton / Dutch Neo-Calvinist / Westminster)**
Westminster codified a bicovenantal structure. The **covenant of works** made with Adam held out eschatological life upon perfect and personal obedience, a probation signified by the tree of life and threatened by death. After the fall, the **covenant of grace** promised salvation in Christ, progressively administered through promise, type, and ordinance, culminating in the **new covenant** with clearer efficacy. Chapter 7 binds this to sacraments as seals, with baptism and the Supper as **federal signs** of union with Christ.

Old Princeton articulated covenant with dogmatic precision and exegetical restraint. **Charles Hodge 1871–1873** defended Adamic **federal representation** in Romans 5 and insisted that justification is a judicial benefit secured by the obedience of the second Adam within the **pactum salutis**. **Geerhardus Vos 1930** in Pauline Eschatology linked covenant to inaugurated eschatology, reading the Mosaic economy as typological and the new covenant as the Spirit’s firstfruits. This stream championed **law-gospel clarity** while welcoming biblical theology.

Dutch Neo-Calvinism integrated covenant with culture and creation grace. **Abraham Kuyper 1898** stressed covenant as the architecture of common and special grace, while **Herman Bavinck 1895–1901** located the **pactum salutis** in the eternal life of the Trinity, safeguarding **Trinitarian missions and processions** while distinguishing the *ad intra* decree from the *ad extra* covenants. Later, **Meredith Kline 1968 and 1994** refined a republication thesis at Sinai that asserted a **typological works principle** for Israel’s theocratic tenure without compromising the single **covenant of grace**.

#### 5.**Alternative Accounts (Roman Catholic / Lutheran / Modern / Analytic)**
Roman Catholic theology receives covenant as the sacramental form of the one economy of salvation. **Thomas Aquinas** treats the New Law as the grace of the Spirit that interiorly inclines the will, which frames a **covenantal ethic of charity** rather than forensic emphasis. Contemporary Catholic voices such as **Joseph Ratzinger** and **Scott Hahn** read Scripture through a sequence of covenants culminating in Eucharistic communion, yielding a robust **sacramental covenantalism** that resists the Reformed law-gospel polarity.

Lutheran theology prizes the **law-gospel distinction** as hermeneutical canon and often views covenant language as liable to moralizing. Classic Lutheran orthodoxy in the **Formula of Concord 1577** rejects any notion of creaturely merit under a prelapsarian works covenant, preferring to speak of Adam’s original righteousness and the gospel’s unconditional promise. Modern confessional Lutherans affirm **justification by faith** while using covenant sparingly to preserve pure promise and avoid conflating **ordo salutis** and history.

Modern revisions vary widely. **Karl Barth 1936–1967** centered covenant within election, claiming that the covenant is the internal basis of creation and that Jesus Christ is both electing God and elected man, which displaces a **covenant of works** and redefines the **pactum salutis** christocentrically. **Dispensationalism** from **Darby to Scofield 1909** separates eras by distinct economies and peoples, while **progressive dispensationalism 1993** narrows the gap with a unified people under inaugurated kingdom yet retains **Israel-church distinction**.

Analytic and biblical-theological proposals seek precision and retrieval. **Oliver Crisp** and **Michael Allen with Scott Swain 2015** have defended the coherence of **federal headship** and the viability of an eternal **covenant of redemption** within **divine simplicity**. **Stephen Wellum and Peter Gentry 2012** propose **progressive covenantalism**, arguing for one plan through multiple covenants culminating in Christ and a regenerate new covenant community, a model that challenges **paedobaptist continuity** while rejecting classical **dispensationalism**.

#### 6.**Current Debates and Objections**
First is the question of Sinai and republication. The **republication debate** asks whether the Mosaic covenant republished the **covenant of works** for Israel’s theocratic life. **Klinean defenders** argue that a typological works principle governed land tenure and sanctuary access, while opponents within confessional Reformed camps fear a compromise of **sola gratia** and unity of the **covenant of grace**. The controversy turns on exegesis of Leviticus 18:5, Deuteronomy 28, and Galatians 3.

Second is the merit and grace problem. Critics often object that any **covenant of works** implies Pelagian self-salvation, yet Reformed responses distinguish prelapsarian **condescending merit** from postlapsarian strict merit, insisting that God’s fatherly covenant offered eschatological life to Adam by gratuitous arrangement. The coherence of **federal headship** is also challenged by individualist assumptions, to which Romans 5 and 1 Corinthians 15 supply a communal ontology of representation under **divine righteousness**.

Third is the shape of the new covenant community. **Paedobaptists** argue that covenant signs track with promise to believers and their children under the unity of the **covenant of grace**, whereas **credobaptists** insist that Jeremiah 31 entails a fully regenerate membership and so restrict baptism to professing believers. Allied debates concern **Federal Vision 2002 onward**, where some introduced objectivist language of union and justification that confessional Reformed theologians judged to blur **justification by faith alone**.

Fourth is Pauline interpretation. The **New Perspective on Paul** since 1977 reads Second Temple Judaism as covenantal nomism rather than merit theology, which recasts Paul’s polemic as boundary markers rather than works righteousness. Reformed covenant theology has partly absorbed this sociological insight while maintaining that Paul opposes any contribution of human obedience to **justification**, using **Abrahamic promise** as the paradigm of grace.

#### 7.**System Links (Trinity / Christology / Anthropology etc.)**
Trinitarianly, the **pactum salutis** names the eternal willing of the Father to give a people to the Son, the Son’s free acceptance of the mediatorial mission, and the Spirit’s role to apply redemption, all in harmony with **Rahners Rule** that the economic missions reveal but do not collapse into the immanent processions. This guards **divine simplicity** by identifying the pact with the one decree while preserving the personal taxis, and it grounds the historical **covenant of grace** in eternal love.

Christologically, Jesus is the **mediator of the new covenant**, the true Israel who fulfills law, bears curse, and rises as second Adam. His active and passive obedience completes the probation forfeited under the **covenant of works**, and his blood ratifies the **new covenant** whose Spirit writes the law on the heart. Anthropologically, image-bearing entails covenantal vocation, so Adamic **federal headship** explains original sin and grace, while Ecclesiology sees the church as the covenant people gathered by **word and sacrament** into one body.

Eschatologically, covenants provide the spine from promise to fulfillment, so the already and not yet of the kingdom is the historical unfolding of the **covenant of grace**. The sacraments as **covenant signs** pledge the future, the Supper as new covenant banquet previews Revelation 19, and baptism as exodus through water anticipates the final **new creation**. Ethics is covenantal participation in Christ’s law of love under **union with Christ** and Spirit-wrought conformity.

#### 8.**Pastoral and Liturgical Payoff**
Pastorally, covenant theology stabilizes assurance by locating salvation in God’s sworn oath and Christ’s finished obedience within the **pactum salutis**. Preaching can rightly divide **law and gospel**, pressing the law’s pedagogical function while holding out the free promise of the **covenant of grace** to the weary. Catechesis can narrate the whole Bible as one promise through many forms, training disciples to read typology and promise in Christ.

Liturgically, the church assembles as a covenant people summoned by **divine speech** and sealed by **covenant signs**. Confession of sin and declaration of pardon follow the shape of **covenant lawsuit and covenant renewal**, while baptism and the Supper enact belonging and communion in the **new covenant**. Such rhythms form a missionary people who embody **new creation ethics** in public and private vocations under the Lordship of the **mediator-king**.

#### 9.**One concise closing sentence.**
From Adam to the Lamb, God binds himself by oath so that through the **covenant of grace** fulfilled by the **mediator of the new covenant**, fallen image-bearers are restored under Christ’s **federal headship** unto new creation.
