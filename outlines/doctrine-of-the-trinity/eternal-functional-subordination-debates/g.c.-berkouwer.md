# G.C. Berkouwer (1903–1996)

G.C. Berkouwer approached the **doctrine of the Trinity** with caution regarding speculative formulations, emphasizing the mystery and unity of the Godhead. In the context of the **Eternal Functional Subordination (EFS) debates**, Berkouwer was critical of any attempt to ground **authority and submission** within the eternal relations of the divine persons, warning against projecting human categories onto the **immanent Trinity**. He advocated for a careful distinction between the **economic** and **immanent Trinity**, resisting any subordinationism within the eternal being of God.

- "The confession of the Trinity does not allow for a subordination of essence or of will within the Godhead." (The Doctrine of the Trinity)

## Key Works

- The Doctrine of the Trinity (De Drie-eenheid)
- The Person of Christ
- Studies in Dogmatics: The Work of Christ
- Studies in Dogmatics: The Providence of God
- Studies in Dogmatics: Holy Scripture

## Key Theological Commitments

1. **Rejection of Eternal Subordination in the Trinity**
    - Berkouwer firmly denied any form of **eternal subordination** among the divine persons.
    - He argued that subordinationist language undermines the **co-equality** and **co-eternity** of Father, Son, and Spirit.
    - He insisted that distinctions in the Trinity are of **relation**, not of rank or authority.
    - Any subordination is strictly limited to the **economic Trinity** and the history of salvation.
    "The Trinity is not a hierarchy but a communion of persons in perfect unity." (The Doctrine of the Trinity)

2. **Distinction Between Economic and Immanent Trinity**
    - Berkouwer maintained a clear distinction between God's **eternal being** and His actions in **redemptive history**.
    - He warned against reading the **missions** of the Son and Spirit back into the eternal relations.
    - The **sending** of the Son is a matter of the economy, not of eternal authority structures.
    - The **immanent Trinity** is characterized by mutual indwelling, not subordination.
    "We must not confuse the economic revelation with the eternal reality of the triune God." (The Doctrine of the Trinity)

3. **Emphasis on the Mystery of the Trinity**
    - Berkouwer stressed the **mystery** and incomprehensibility of the triune God.
    - He cautioned against speculative attempts to explain intra-Trinitarian relationships.
    - The unity and diversity of the Trinity must be confessed, not rationalized.
    - Human analogies and categories are inadequate for describing the divine life.
    "The mystery of the Trinity resists all human attempts at systematization." (The Doctrine of the Trinity)

4. **Critique of Subordinationist Tendencies in Church History**
    - Berkouwer analyzed historical debates and rejected **Arian** and **semi-Arian** tendencies.
    - He affirmed the **Nicene** doctrine of homoousios as safeguarding the equality of the persons.
    - He viewed any eternal subordination as a threat to orthodox **Trinitarianism**.
    - The church’s confessions must be protected from hierarchical distortions.
    "The church has always resisted any teaching that would compromise the full deity of the Son and the Spirit." (The Doctrine of the Trinity)

5. **Christological Implications for Trinitarian Doctrine**
    - Berkouwer argued that Christ’s **obedience** pertains to His incarnate mission, not to His eternal personhood.
    - The **kenosis** and submission of Christ are economic, not ontological.
    - He warned that EFS confuses the **incarnation** with eternal relations.
    - Proper Christology upholds both the full deity and true humanity of Christ without introducing subordination into the Godhead.
    "The obedience of Christ is the obedience of the incarnate Son, not of the eternal Logos." (The Person of Christ)

## Legacy and Impact

Berkouwer’s careful distinction between the **economic** and **immanent Trinity** has shaped contemporary Reformed responses to the EFS debates. His insistence on the **co-equality** of the divine persons and his critique of speculative subordinationism have provided a robust theological framework for upholding orthodox Trinitarian doctrine. Berkouwer’s influence endures in systematic theology, especially among those wary of importing human authority structures into the eternal life of God.
